**HubSpot** is the CRM that sales reps actually use. Clean interface, great onboarding, and everything connects across marketing, sales, and service. You pay a premium for that polish.

**Zoho** is the value play. A full-featured CRM with 55+ integrated apps at prices that make HubSpot look extravagant. A 10-person team on Zoho Enterprise pays $400/mo. The same team on HubSpot? $1,500+.

## Pick HubSpot if...

You're a B2B company that relies on inbound marketing and content. You value a polished UX and fast team adoption over cost savings. Your budget can handle the Professional tier ($100/seat/mo), which is where HubSpot's real power lives.

## Pick Zoho if...

You're a cost-conscious SMB that needs a full-featured CRM without enterprise pricing. You want deep customization and you're willing to invest time configuring things. You already use (or plan to use) other Zoho apps, which makes the ecosystem integration a huge bonus.

## The details

**Pricing:** For a 5-person team on annual billing, HubSpot entry is $100/mo vs Zoho's $70/mo. At mid-tier, it's $500/mo (HubSpot Pro) vs $115/mo (Zoho Pro). At enterprise, $750/mo vs $200/mo. And HubSpot's Marketing Hub is priced separately by contact volume, starting at $800/mo for 2K contacts. Zoho's marketing automation starts at $15/mo. HubSpot also charges mandatory onboarding fees ($1,500-$3,000+) on Professional and Enterprise. Zoho doesn't.

**Features:** HubSpot wins on marketing automation, user experience, and reporting dashboards. Zoho wins on customization depth, the 55+ app ecosystem, and the Blueprint feature for structured sales processes (more sophisticated than anything HubSpot offers natively). Both have AI assistants. HubSpot's feels more polished. Zoho's Zia has been around longer.

**Ease of use:** HubSpot wins. The interface is clean, modern, and consistent. New users navigate it intuitively. Zoho is functional but busier. The depth of customization can overwhelm new users. For teams that value simplicity and fast onboarding, HubSpot is the easier choice.

**Scalability:** Zoho scales more gracefully on cost. A 50-person team on Zoho Enterprise is $2,000/mo vs $7,500+ on HubSpot. Downgrading HubSpot is painful because workflows and automations break when you drop a tier.

## FAQ

**Is HubSpot better than Zoho?** HubSpot has a better UX and stronger marketing automation. Zoho offers far better value and deeper customization. For most small businesses, Zoho delivers 80% of HubSpot's functionality at 30% of the cost.

**Is HubSpot's free plan worth it?** Absolutely. It's one of the best free CRMs available. The catch is the 2-user limit and the temptation to upgrade as you hit walls, which is exactly HubSpot's growth strategy.

**Can I switch from HubSpot to Zoho?** Yes. Zoho has import wizards for contacts, deals, and notes. Custom workflows need manual recreation. Plan 2-4 weeks for a full migration.
