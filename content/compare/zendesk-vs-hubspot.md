**Zendesk** is the purpose-built support platform for teams handling high ticket volumes. Deeper ticketing, skills-based routing, SLA management, and more automation options. It does one thing and does it well.

**HubSpot Service Hub** is the right call if you already use HubSpot CRM. Your support agents see the full customer journey (marketing emails, sales calls, deals, past tickets) in one view. The free tier and $15/seat entry make it easy to start.

| | **Zendesk** | **HubSpot Service Hub** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $55/agent/mo (Suite Team) | $15/seat/mo (Starter) |
| **Free tier** | No (14-day trial) | Yes (basic ticketing + shared inbox) |
| **Best for** | Dedicated support teams, high volume | SMBs using HubSpot CRM |
| **Standout feature** | Advanced ticketing + SLA management | Unified CRM context across all hubs |
| **Knowledge base** | All paid plans | Professional ($90/seat) and up |
| **AI pricing** | $1-2 per AI-resolved ticket | Included in plan price |

## Pick Zendesk if...

You have a dedicated support team handling high volumes and need deep workflow customization. You want skills-based routing, SLA escalation paths, and a sophisticated macro system. You need a knowledge base on every paid plan. Support quality is a competitive advantage for your business.

## The details

**Pricing:** HubSpot is much cheaper at entry ($15 vs $55 per seat). At mid-tier, they're nearly identical ($90 vs $89 per seat). Watch out: HubSpot Professional requires a 5-seat minimum ($450/mo) and onboarding fees can add $1,500-8,000. Zendesk has no seat minimums and no mandatory onboarding fees.

**Ticketing depth:** Zendesk is the deeper system. Custom ticket fields, conditional logic, skills-based routing, side conversations, SLA escalation, and a powerful macro system. HubSpot handles standard ticketing well but is less configurable for complex support operations.

**Knowledge base:** Zendesk includes it on all Suite plans with better content management, SEO, and multilingual support. HubSpot locks it behind Professional ($90/seat), which is a real limitation for smaller teams.

**CRM context:** HubSpot's killer feature. When a ticket comes in, agents see the customer's full history across marketing, sales, and support. Zendesk can integrate with CRMs but never matches the native experience. If your support agents need business context, HubSpot is hard to beat.

**AI:** Both are investing heavily. Zendesk charges $1-2 per AI-resolved ticket, which favors low volumes but hurts high-volume teams. HubSpot includes AI (Breeze) in the plan price for more predictable costs. Zendesk's AI is more mature for support-specific tasks like ticket classification and routing.

## FAQ

**Can HubSpot replace Zendesk?** For small to mid-size teams with standard needs, yes. For large operations with complex routing, SLAs, and high-volume automation, Zendesk is still more capable.

**Do I need the full HubSpot suite?** No. Service Hub works standalone with the free CRM. But the real value (unified customer data) only kicks in if you use multiple hubs.

**Which has better AI?** Zendesk is more mature for support tasks. HubSpot's AI benefits from cross-hub context. The pricing difference matters: per-resolution vs included in plan.
