## What Is Proton VPN?

Proton VPN is the VPN service from Proton AG, the Swiss company behind ProtonMail. Every app is open-source and independently audited — you can verify the "no logs" claim yourself rather than trusting marketing. Swiss headquarters means strong privacy laws, outside Five Eyes and Fourteen Eyes alliances.

The free tier is Proton VPN's standout feature: unlimited data, no ads, and servers in five locations. No other major VPN offers a free plan this generous, funded by paid subscribers rather than data harvesting.

Paid plans (from $4.49/month on a 2-year plan) unlock 6,500+ servers in 110+ countries, Secure Core (multi-hop routing), NetShield ad/tracker blocking, port forwarding, and streaming optimization. Proton also integrates with Proton Mail, Drive, Calendar, and Pass — the Unlimited bundle ($7.99/month) covers everything.

## What Is NordVPN?

NordVPN is one of the most popular VPN services globally, with over 14 million users. Operated by Nord Security (headquartered in Panama), it has built its reputation on speed, reliability, and a massive server infrastructure.

NordVPN operates 7,400+ servers across 118 countries — one of the largest networks in the industry. Its proprietary NordLynx protocol, built on WireGuard, consistently delivers some of the fastest speeds in independent testing. In 2026, NordVPN added full post-quantum encryption support to NordLynx, implementing NIST-standard algorithms that protect against future quantum computing threats.

The feature set is extensive: Threat Protection (malware/tracker blocking), Meshnet (encrypted P2P connections), Dark Web Monitor, SmartDNS for streaming devices, Double VPN, and specialty servers including dedicated IP, P2P-optimized, and obfuscated options.

NordVPN offers four subscription tiers — Basic (VPN only), Plus (adds Threat Protection and password manager), Complete (adds encrypted cloud storage), and Prime (adds identity theft insurance). The Basic 2-year plan starts at $3.09/month.

Both offer 30-day money-back guarantees. NordVPN's 2-year Basic plan is $1.40/month cheaper than Proton's equivalent. However, NordVPN's Plus plan ($3.89/mo for 2 years), which includes features closer to Proton's paid offering, narrows the gap.

### Speed

NordVPN is consistently faster in independent speed tests. NordLynx delivers average download speeds around 770-780 Mbps in testing, among the fastest in the industry. Proton VPN is fast — comfortably in the top tier — but typically 10-20% behind NordVPN in raw throughput.

For most users, both are fast enough that speed differences are imperceptible during normal browsing, streaming, and downloading. The gap matters more for users with gigabit connections or those who need maximum throughput for large file transfers.

### Privacy and Trust

This is where Proton VPN has a structural advantage.

**Proton VPN:** Open-source apps on all platforms, independent audits published publicly, Swiss jurisdiction, Secure Core servers, and a demonstrated track record of defending user privacy in court.

**NordVPN:** Closed-source (except Linux), multiple independent audits (PwC, Deloitte, Cure53), Panama jurisdiction, and a proven no-logs policy. NordVPN had a 2018 server breach that raised concerns, though no user data was exposed and the company has since moved to RAM-only servers.

Both maintain genuine no-logs policies. Proton's open-source approach offers higher verifiability; NordVPN's relies on trusting independent auditors.

### Streaming

**NordVPN** is the better choice for streaming. It reliably unblocks Netflix (multiple regions), Disney+, BBC iPlayer, Amazon Prime Video, Hulu, and most major streaming platforms. SmartDNS extends this to devices like smart TVs and gaming consoles that do not support VPN apps.

**Proton VPN** handles major streaming services well on paid plans, including Netflix, Disney+, and Amazon Prime. It is reliable but unblocks fewer regional libraries and niche streaming services compared to NordVPN. The free tier does not support streaming.

### Server Network and Extra Features

NordVPN has more servers (7,400+ vs 6,500+) and slightly broader country coverage (118 vs 110+). Both networks are large enough that availability is never a practical concern. NordVPN offers more specialty server types — dedicated IP, Double VPN, obfuscated, and P2P-optimized.

Proton VPN's unique Secure Core routes traffic through hardened servers in Switzerland, Iceland, or Sweden before the exit server — an extra layer against network-level attacks. Proton also integrates with its broader privacy ecosystem (Mail, Drive, Calendar, Pass).

NordVPN bundles more services into premium tiers (password manager, encrypted storage, identity protection). The right choice depends on whether you want a security suite (NordVPN) or a privacy suite (Proton).

## Who Should Use What?

**Pick Proton VPN if:** privacy is your top priority and you value open-source transparency, Swiss jurisdiction, and a company with a proven track record of defending user rights. Also the clear choice if you want a free VPN that is trustworthy and has no data caps. Users in the Proton ecosystem (Mail, Drive, Pass) get additional value from the integrated bundle.

**Pick NordVPN if:** you want the fastest speeds, best streaming access, and the most features in a single subscription. NordVPN is the better all-around VPN for users who want reliable performance, strong security, and a broad feature set without needing to verify the source code themselves.

## FAQ

### Is Proton VPN's free tier safe?

Yes. Proton's free tier is funded by paid subscribers, not ads or data harvesting. It uses the same encryption and no-logs policy as paid plans. The limitations are speed (lower priority on servers), locations (5 countries), and no streaming support — but there are no privacy compromises.

### Which is better for torrenting?

Both support P2P traffic on designated servers. NordVPN has more P2P-optimized servers and faster speeds. Proton VPN offers port forwarding on paid plans, which can improve torrent connectivity. Both are solid choices.

For most users, **NordVPN** is the better all-around VPN. It is faster, unblocks more streaming services, and packs more features into its subscription tiers. The price is competitive, and the security credentials are strong.

For privacy-conscious users who want maximum transparency, **Proton VPN** is the better choice. Open-source code, Swiss jurisdiction, and the Proton ecosystem make it the most trustworthy VPN option available. The free tier also makes it the best entry point for anyone who has never used a VPN before.
