## The details

**Pricing:** ActiveCampaign looks cheaper at entry ($15 vs $19 for 1K contacts), but the gap flips fast. At 10K contacts, GetResponse is $79/mo while ActiveCampaign is $149/mo. Nearly double. And ActiveCampaign's real power lives on the Plus plan ($189/mo at 10K contacts). GetResponse includes unlimited sends on all plans.

**Automation:** ActiveCampaign is in a different league. 135+ triggers vs GetResponse's more modest set, plus conditional logic, split actions, goal tracking, and predictive sending. If automation is your whole strategy, ActiveCampaign justifies the premium.

**Features you get with GetResponse:** Built-in webinar hosting (live, evergreen, paid), 500+ email templates, conversion funnels, and an AI email generator. ActiveCampaign has none of these. If webinars are part of your funnel, GetResponse saves you a separate subscription.

**Ease of use:** GetResponse is more approachable. Cleaner interface, guided onboarding, you can be sending campaigns within an hour. ActiveCampaign's power comes with complexity. Plan for a week or two to feel comfortable, especially with the automation builder.

**Deliverability:** Both are strong, consistently in the 90%+ inbox placement range. ActiveCampaign has a slight edge in independent tests thanks to aggressive list hygiene tools.

## FAQ

**Is GetResponse better than ActiveCampaign?** Depends what you need. GetResponse gives you more features per dollar. ActiveCampaign gives you deeper automation and a real CRM. Price and breadth vs depth and power.

**Which has better deliverability?** Both are excellent. ActiveCampaign has a slight edge in most independent tests. Neither will be your bottleneck.

**Can I switch between them?** Yes. Both support CSV import/export. ActiveCampaign offers free migration on higher plans. Budget a few days to recreate complex automations.
