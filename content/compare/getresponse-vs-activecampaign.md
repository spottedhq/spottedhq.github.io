## What Is GetResponse?

GetResponse is one of the longest-running email marketing platforms on the market, founded in 1998 in Poland and now serving over 400,000 businesses in 183 countries. What started as a simple autoresponder service has grown into a comprehensive marketing platform that tries to be the only tool you need.

The core appeal of GetResponse is breadth. On a single platform you get email marketing, marketing automation, landing pages, conversion funnels, webinar hosting, an AI website builder, e-commerce tools, and even SMS marketing. For small and mid-sized businesses that do not want to pay for -- and manage -- six different subscriptions, this all-in-one approach is genuinely attractive.

Pricing is structured across four plans: Starter ($19/mo), Marketer ($59/mo), Creator ($69/mo), and the enterprise MAX plan (starting at $1,099/mo). All plans include unlimited email sends, and you can save up to 30% by committing to a 24-month billing cycle. GetResponse also includes an AI email generator and subject line optimizer on all paid plans.

## What Is ActiveCampaign?

ActiveCampaign is a marketing automation and CRM platform that has earned a reputation as one of the most powerful email tools available -- particularly when it comes to automation complexity. Founded in 2003, the platform now serves over 180,000 businesses and is a favorite among marketing teams that need granular control over their customer journeys.

Where ActiveCampaign truly excels is automation depth. The visual automation builder supports 135+ triggers and actions, with 500+ pre-built automation recipes categorized by industry. You can build multi-step, multi-channel workflows that span email, SMS, site messaging, and CRM updates -- all from one canvas. This is not just autoresponder-level automation; it is genuine marketing orchestration.

ActiveCampaign offers four plans: Starter ($15/mo for 1,000 contacts), Plus ($49/mo), Pro ($79/mo), and Enterprise ($145/mo). Pricing scales with contact count, and the jump between tiers is significant. At 5,000 contacts, you are looking at $79/mo (Starter), $145/mo (Plus), $205/mo (Pro), or $375/mo (Enterprise). Annual billing saves roughly 20%. Be aware that add-ons for SMS, custom reporting, and transactional email can push real costs 30-50% above headline prices.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Pricing

At the entry level, ActiveCampaign looks cheaper -- $15/mo vs $19/mo for 1,000 contacts. But this comparison is misleading because ActiveCampaign's Starter plan is more limited than GetResponse's Starter.

| List Size | **GetResponse (Starter)** | **ActiveCampaign (Starter)** | **ActiveCampaign (Plus)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1,000 contacts | $19/mo | $15/mo | $49/mo |
| 2,500 contacts | $29/mo | $39/mo | $95/mo |
| 5,000 contacts | $49/mo | $79/mo | $145/mo |
| 10,000 contacts | $79/mo | $149/mo | $189/mo |

The story flips quickly as your list grows. At 10,000 contacts, GetResponse Starter is $79/mo while ActiveCampaign Starter is $149/mo -- nearly double. If you need ActiveCampaign's Plus features (which is where the platform really shines), you are paying $189/mo at that list size. GetResponse is consistently the more affordable option, especially for growing businesses.

### Features

**Automation:** ActiveCampaign is the clear winner here. Its automation builder is in a different league -- 135+ triggers versus GetResponse's more modest set, plus conditional logic, split actions, goal tracking, and predictive sending. If automation is the core of your marketing strategy, ActiveCampaign justifies its higher price.

**Email design:** GetResponse offers 500+ pre-designed templates and a polished drag-and-drop editor. ActiveCampaign's editor is functional but has fewer templates and a steeper learning curve. For teams without a designer, GetResponse makes it easier to produce good-looking emails quickly.

**Webinars:** GetResponse includes built-in webinar hosting on its Marketer plan -- live, evergreen, and paid webinars. ActiveCampaign has no webinar feature. If webinars are part of your funnel, GetResponse saves you a separate Zoom or Webex subscription.

**CRM:** ActiveCampaign includes a capable built-in CRM with deal pipelines, lead scoring, and win probability on its Plus plan and above. GetResponse's CRM features are more basic and only available on higher tiers. For sales-driven teams, ActiveCampaign's CRM is a genuine differentiator.

**Integrations:** ActiveCampaign connects with 900+ tools versus GetResponse's 170+. If your tech stack is complex, ActiveCampaign is more likely to have native integrations with what you already use.

**Deliverability:** Both platforms invest heavily in deliverability. ActiveCampaign consistently ranks among the top platforms in independent deliverability tests, with strong list hygiene tools, spam testing, and dedicated IP options on higher plans. GetResponse also performs well, typically landing in the 90%+ inbox placement range.

### Ease of Use

GetResponse is the more approachable platform. The interface is cleaner, the onboarding is guided, and most features are discoverable without reading documentation. You can be sending campaigns within your first hour.

ActiveCampaign has a steeper learning curve. The sheer number of options in the automation builder, CRM, and settings can overwhelm new users. This is the tradeoff for its power -- you get more control, but you need more time to learn the system. Plan for a week or two to feel fully comfortable, especially if you are building complex automations.

## Who Should Use What?

**Pick GetResponse if:** you want an all-in-one platform that covers email, landing pages, webinars, and basic automation at a price that does not balloon as your list grows. It is the smarter choice for SMBs, e-commerce businesses, and solo marketers who need broad functionality without the complexity tax.

**Pick ActiveCampaign if:** automation is the beating heart of your marketing. If you are building multi-step customer journeys across email, SMS, and your website -- and you need a real CRM with deal pipelines and lead scoring -- ActiveCampaign is worth the premium. It is the go-to for B2B sales teams, agencies, and marketing-mature companies that have outgrown simpler tools.

## FAQ

### Is GetResponse better than ActiveCampaign?

It depends entirely on what you prioritize. GetResponse offers better value and more features per dollar -- webinars, landing pages, and unlimited sends are included where ActiveCampaign would require add-ons or integrations. But ActiveCampaign's automation and CRM capabilities are objectively more advanced. If you are choosing on price and breadth, GetResponse wins. If you are choosing on automation depth and sales pipeline management, ActiveCampaign wins.

### Can I switch from GetResponse to ActiveCampaign?

Yes. Both platforms support CSV imports and exports. ActiveCampaign offers a free migration service on higher-tier plans that will transfer your contacts, templates, and even recreate simple automations. Moving complex automation workflows manually takes time -- budget a few days for a full migration if you have been on either platform for a while.

### Which has better deliverability?

Both are strong. ActiveCampaign has a slight edge in most independent deliverability tests, partly because of its aggressive list hygiene tools and machine-learning-based send-time optimization. GetResponse is close behind and offers solid deliverability infrastructure across all plans. Neither platform will be the bottleneck in your email program.

## Our Recommendation

For most small businesses and solo marketers, GetResponse delivers more value. You get webinars, landing pages, automation, and unlimited sends in one package at a price that stays manageable as you scale. Choose ActiveCampaign when you have genuinely outgrown simpler automation tools and need the depth -- complex conditional workflows, CRM deal management, and cross-channel orchestration. Just budget for the true cost, not just the headline price.
