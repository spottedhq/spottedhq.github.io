**Mailchimp** is the all-in-one email marketing platform for e-commerce and SMEs. Deep Shopify/WooCommerce integrations, 100+ templates, and a powerful automation builder. It costs more and counts unsubscribed contacts against your limit.

**Kit (ConvertKit)** is the email platform creators love. 10,000 free subscribers, built-in paid newsletters and digital product sales, and a tagging system that actually makes sense. Less flashy, more effective for audience building.

| | **Mailchimp** | **Kit (ConvertKit)** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $13/mo (Essentials, 500 contacts) | $39/mo (Creator, 1,000 subs) |
| **Free tier** | 250 contacts, 500 emails/mo | 10,000 subscribers, unlimited emails |
| **Best for** | E-commerce, design-heavy campaigns | Creators, bloggers, course sellers |
| **Standout feature** | Customer Journey Builder | Built-in monetization (paid subs, digital products) |
| **Templates** | 100+ polished designs | Minimal (text-first by design) |
| **Creator monetization** | None | Paid newsletters, tip jars, creator network |

You run an online store and need abandoned cart emails, product recommendations, and purchase-based automations. You want polished visual templates and an all-in-one marketing dashboard.

## The details

**Pricing:** Kit's free plan is wildly more generous (10,000 subs vs 250 contacts). On paid plans, Mailchimp is cheaper per contact at scale. But Mailchimp counts unsubscribed contacts toward your limit unless you manually archive them. That hidden cost catches a lot of people.

**Email quality:** Mailchimp gives you drag-and-drop templates that look polished out of the box. Kit's emails are deliberately plain-text-focused. That's not a bug. Simple emails feel personal and get higher engagement, especially for creator audiences.

**Automation:** Mailchimp's Customer Journey Builder handles complex branching workflows, but the best features are locked behind the Standard plan ($20/mo+). Kit's visual automations are simpler but cover the sequences creators actually need: welcome series, product launches, webinar funnels.

**The real difference:** Mailchimp is a marketing platform. Kit is a creator platform. Mailchimp helps you sell products through a store. Kit helps you sell directly to your audience through your email list.

## FAQ

**Is Kit's free plan actually good?** Yes. 10,000 subscribers with unlimited emails, landing pages, and forms. You only get one automation on free, but that's enough to start.

**Can I migrate from Mailchimp to Kit?** Yes. Kit has a built-in migration tool. Under 10,000 subscribers takes about an hour.

**Which has better deliverability?** Both are strong. Kit's smaller, creator-focused user base tends to produce slightly better deliverability rates. Text-based emails also dodge spam filters more effectively.
