## The details

**Pricing:** Not even close. Carrd Pro Standard at $19/year costs less than one month of Leadpages Standard at $49/mo. Over a year, Leadpages is $444 (annual billing) vs Carrd's $19. That's a 23x difference. But the comparison is only fair on the surface. Leadpages includes A/B testing, conversion analytics, and AI optimization that Carrd doesn't have.

**What you get:** Leadpages gives you 200+ conversion-optimized templates, split testing, Leadmeter AI that scores your page before you publish, pop-ups, alert bars, and analytics dashboards. Carrd gives you beautiful one-page sites, custom domains, forms (integrated with Mailchimp, Kit, HubSpot, etc.), and Stripe/PayPal payments. Carrd Pro Plus ($49/year) even lets you download your site's source code.

**Ease of use:** Carrd is one of the easiest website builders ever made. Pick a template, swap content, publish. Most people are done in 15-20 minutes. Leadpages has more options (conversion widgets, payment forms, countdown timers, pop-up triggers), so it takes longer to learn. A few hours to feel comfortable.

**The smart path:** Start with Carrd to validate the idea. Graduate to Leadpages when conversion optimization is worth the investment.

## FAQ

**Can Carrd replace Leadpages?** For simple landing pages with a signup form, yes. For A/B testing, conversion analytics, and AI optimization, no. If your landing page generates revenue through paid traffic, Leadpages pays for itself.

**Does Carrd support e-commerce?** Payment forms via Stripe and PayPal, yes. Shopping cart, inventory, product catalog, no. For real e-commerce you need a different tool.

**Can I build a multi-page site with either?** No. Leadpages builds individual landing pages (you can link them together). Carrd is strictly one-page. For a full website, look at WordPress, Webflow, or Squarespace.
