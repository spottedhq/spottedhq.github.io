## Quick Verdict

Choosing between **Webflow** and **Squarespace**? Here's the short version: **Webflow** is best for designers and developers who want pixel-perfect control and clean exported code. **Squarespace** shines for non-technical users who want a beautiful site up and running in an afternoon. They serve fundamentally different audiences despite both being "website builders."

## What Is Webflow?

Webflow is a visual web design tool that generates clean, production-ready HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Think of it as a professional design tool (like Figma) crossed with a hosting platform. It launched in 2013 and has become the go-to platform for designers and agencies who want full creative control without writing code by hand.

What makes Webflow unique is the depth of its visual editor. You are not dragging pre-made blocks around -- you are manipulating actual CSS properties through a visual interface. Flexbox, CSS Grid, custom animations, interactions triggered by scroll position -- Webflow exposes nearly the full power of modern web design through its designer tool. The result is sites that look and perform like custom-coded projects.

Webflow also has a capable CMS that supports dynamic content, collections, and filtering -- making it suitable for blogs, directories, and content-heavy sites. And unlike Squarespace, Webflow lets you export your code if you ever want to host elsewhere. In 2026, Webflow's site plans start at $18/mo for Basic (no CMS), with CMS plans and Business plans scaling up from there. There is also a free Starter plan limited to a webflow.io subdomain and 2 pages.

## What Is Squarespace?

Squarespace is the website builder that made beautiful design accessible to everyone. Founded in 2003 and publicly traded since 2021, it powers millions of websites and is particularly popular with creatives, small business owners, restaurants, and portfolio sites.

Squarespace's strength is its template system. You pick from dozens of professionally designed, mobile-responsive templates and customize them through a structured editor. You cannot break the design because the editor constrains you to choices that always look good. For someone who does not know CSS from HTML, this is a feature, not a limitation.

In 2026, Squarespace rolled out a new four-plan structure: Basic ($16/mo), Core ($23/mo), Plus ($39/mo), and Advanced ($99/mo). All plans now include e-commerce capabilities, which is a significant change from previous years when e-commerce required upgrading to Business or Commerce plans. Squarespace does not have a free tier but offers a 14-day free trial on all plans. A free custom domain for the first year is included with annual billing.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

**Squarespace Plans (annual billing):**
| Plan | Price | Key features |
|---|---|---|
| Basic | $16/mo | Simple sites, blogs, basic e-commerce |
| Core | $23/mo | Advanced analytics, e-commerce features |

For a standard business website, Squarespace is cheaper at the entry level ($16 vs $18) and includes more features out of the box. For e-commerce, Webflow's plans start at $29/mo with a 2% transaction fee on Standard, while Squarespace includes e-commerce on all plans (transaction fees vary by tier).

Where Webflow gets expensive is the workspace layer. If you work on client projects, Webflow's Core workspace plan costs $19/mo (annual) and is required for code export and managing multiple projects. Agencies and freelancers should budget for both site plans and workspace plans.

### Design and Customization

**Webflow** gives you near-unlimited design freedom. You can build any layout, animation, or interaction without touching code. The visual editor maps directly to CSS properties, so experienced designers feel right at home. Custom fonts, precise spacing, scroll-triggered animations, Lottie integrations -- it is all possible without plugins.

**Squarespace** offers beautiful but constrained design. You work within template structures that ensure everything looks polished. The drag-and-drop editor is intuitive, and Squarespace's design quality floor is high -- even a first-timer will produce something that looks professional. But you will hit walls if you want something the template does not support.

The gap is real: Webflow is for people who want to design a website. Squarespace is for people who want to have a website.

### Content Management

Webflow's CMS is powerful and flexible. You define custom content structures (collections), create dynamic pages that pull from those collections, and filter/sort content visually. It is excellent for blogs, directories, job boards, and any content-driven site.

Squarespace's CMS is simpler and more opinionated. Blog posts, products, events, and gallery pages all have dedicated content types with pre-built layouts. It is faster to set up but less flexible. If your content fits Squarespace's built-in types, it works beautifully. If you need custom data structures, you will be frustrated.

### E-commerce

Squarespace has the more mature e-commerce system for small sellers. Product pages, inventory management, shipping calculations, tax handling, and customer accounts are all built in. The new 2026 plan structure means every Squarespace plan supports e-commerce, though processing fees decrease as you move up tiers.

Webflow's e-commerce is capable but pricier and less feature-rich. It is best suited for design-forward product showcases and smaller catalogs. For businesses with complex shipping needs, extensive product variants, or high order volumes, Squarespace or a dedicated platform like Shopify is a better fit.

### Ease of Use

Squarespace wins here, hands down. A non-technical user can sign up and have a professional-looking website live within a few hours. The template selection, guided setup, and structured editor make it nearly impossible to produce an ugly result.

Webflow has a significant learning curve. Even experienced designers need a week or two to become comfortable with the editor. The payoff is enormous creative freedom, but the investment is real. Webflow offers Webflow University (free video courses), which is excellent, but you should plan for a ramp-up period.

### SEO

Both platforms handle the basics well -- custom meta titles, descriptions, alt text, clean URLs, and automatic sitemaps. Webflow gives you more granular control over page structure, heading hierarchy, and schema markup. Squarespace keeps SEO simple with built-in tools that handle the fundamentals without requiring technical knowledge.

## Who Should Use What?

**Pick Webflow if:** you are a designer, developer, or agency building custom websites for clients. You want full creative control, clean code export, and a powerful CMS. You are willing to invest time learning the platform in exchange for capabilities that rival hand-coded sites.

**Pick Squarespace if:** you are a small business owner, creative professional, or anyone who needs a polished website without a steep learning curve. You want beautiful templates, built-in e-commerce, and a platform that just works. You value speed of launch over design perfection.

## FAQ

### Is Webflow better than Squarespace?

Webflow is more powerful and flexible. Squarespace is easier and faster. Neither is objectively "better" -- they are built for different users. A designer will feel limited by Squarespace. A bakery owner will feel overwhelmed by Webflow. Choose based on your skills and needs.

### Can I switch from Squarespace to Webflow?

There is no automatic migration path. You would need to rebuild your site design in Webflow and manually move your content. Blog posts can be exported from Squarespace as XML and imported into Webflow's CMS, but product data, pages, and design do not transfer.

### Which is better for SEO?

Both are solid for SEO fundamentals. Webflow offers more technical control (custom code injection, detailed schema markup, finer HTML structure). Squarespace handles the basics well out of the box. For most small business sites, the SEO difference is negligible -- content quality matters far more than platform choice.

### Do I need to know code to use Webflow?

No, but understanding CSS concepts (flexbox, margins, padding, responsive breakpoints) will dramatically accelerate your learning. Webflow abstracts away the code-writing but exposes the underlying logic. Pure beginners can learn through Webflow University, but expect a steeper ramp-up than Squarespace.
