**Webflow** is for designers and agencies who want pixel-perfect control over every CSS property, a powerful CMS, and clean code export. It builds sites that rival custom-coded projects. The learning curve is 2-4 weeks.

**Squarespace** is for small business owners and creatives who want a professional site live in an afternoon. Beautiful templates, built-in e-commerce on every plan, and you can't accidentally make it ugly. No code knowledge needed.

## Pick Webflow if...

You're a designer or agency building custom sites for clients. You need a flexible CMS for blogs, directories, or resource libraries. You want code export. You're willing to invest weeks learning the platform in exchange for near-unlimited design freedom.

## The details

**Pricing:** Nearly identical at entry level ($18 vs $16). Squarespace includes e-commerce on all plans. Webflow's e-commerce starts at $29/mo with a 2% transaction fee. Where Webflow gets expensive is the workspace layer. Agencies need a Core workspace ($19/mo) on top of site plans for code export and multi-project management.

**Design freedom:** Webflow gives you control over every CSS property. Flexbox, grid, custom animations, scroll interactions. Squarespace constrains you to template structures that always look good. The gap is real: Webflow is for people who want to design a website. Squarespace is for people who want to have a website.

**CMS:** Webflow's CMS lets you define custom content structures, create dynamic pages, and filter content visually. Squarespace's CMS is simpler with pre-built types (blog, products, events). If your content fits Squarespace's types, it works well. If you need custom data structures, you'll hit walls.

**E-commerce:** Squarespace has the more mature system for small sellers. Products, inventory, shipping, taxes, and customer accounts are all built in on every plan. Webflow's e-commerce is capable but pricier and less feature-rich. For complex catalogs, neither beats Shopify.

**Ease of use:** Squarespace wins, hands down. A non-technical user can have a professional site live in hours. Webflow takes weeks of practice. The payoff is more creative freedom, but the investment is real.

## FAQ

**Is Webflow better than Squarespace?** Webflow is more powerful. Squarespace is easier. A designer will feel limited by Squarespace. A bakery owner will feel overwhelmed by Webflow. Choose based on your skills.

**Can I migrate from Squarespace to Webflow?** No automated path. You'd rebuild the design and manually move content. Blog posts export as XML, but pages and products don't transfer.

**Which is better for SEO?** Both handle fundamentals well. Webflow gives more technical control (schema markup, custom sitemaps, code injection). For most small business sites, the SEO difference is negligible.
