**Zapier** is for people who want simple automations running in minutes. 7,000+ app integrations, a linear step-by-step builder, and you can connect two apps without reading docs. It gets expensive fast at scale.

**Make** (formerly Integromat) is for people who think visually and need more control. A drag-and-drop canvas with branching, looping, and error handling. 13x more operations per dollar than Zapier on entry-level plans.

| | **Zapier** | **Make** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $19.99/mo (Professional) | $10.59/mo (Core) |
| **Free tier** | 100 tasks/mo | 1,000 operations/mo |
| **Best for** | Simple 2-3 step automations | Complex multi-branch workflows |
| **Standout feature** | 7,000+ app integrations | Visual canvas builder + 13x value/dollar |
| **Branching logic** | Paid plans only | All plans including free |
| **AI features** | Natural language automation builder | AI agents on all paid plans |

## Pick Zapier if...

You need simple, reliable automations between popular apps. You're non-technical and want something running in 5 minutes. Your workflows are mostly trigger-action pairs (new lead comes in, send to CRM, notify Slack). You need integrations with niche or industry-specific apps that Make doesn't support.

## Pick Make if...

You're building workflows with conditions, loops, and multiple branches. Budget matters. You're comfortable with a visual builder and willing to spend an afternoon learning it. You need advanced data transformation, API calls, or per-module error handling.

## The details

**Pricing:** Make gives you roughly 13x more operations per dollar on entry-level paid plans ($10.59 for 10,000 ops vs $19.99 for 750 tasks). The gap widens at scale. Heavy Zapier users easily hit $100-300/mo. Make's Core plan handles most of that at $10.59.

**App library:** Zapier has 7,000+ integrations vs Make's 2,000+. If you need a niche app, Zapier is more likely to have it. For popular tools (Google, Slack, HubSpot, Stripe), both cover you.

**Workflow complexity:** Make wins. The visual canvas shows your entire automation as a flowchart. Routers split paths, iterators loop through arrays, and you see the logic. Zapier's linear builder works for simple chains but gets awkward with branching. Zapier restricts Paths to paid plans; Make includes routers and filters on free.

**Ease of use:** Zapier is easier for beginners. Step-by-step prompts guide you through trigger-action pairs. Make's canvas is powerful but overwhelming at first. Give it a weekend and it becomes faster for anything complex. The initial investment is real.

**AI features:** Both are investing heavily. Zapier builds automations from natural language descriptions. Make includes AI agents on all paid plans and lets you plug in your own API keys (OpenAI, Anthropic). For custom AI pipelines, Make's visual builder is more flexible.

## FAQ

**Is Make really that much cheaper?** Yes. 13x more operations per dollar on entry plans. The gap narrows at enterprise tiers but Make stays cheaper at every level.

**Can I migrate from Zapier to Make?** No one-click tool. You rebuild automations manually, typically a few hours of work. Many users say their workflows improve in the process.

**Do I need to be a developer for Make?** No, but it helps to think systematically. If you've built a complex spreadsheet formula, you can learn Make.
