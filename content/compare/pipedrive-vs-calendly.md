## The details

**Pricing:** Different categories. Pipedrive is a full CRM ($14-79/seat/mo). Calendly is a focused scheduler (free to $16/seat/mo). If you only need scheduling, Calendly is cheaper. If you need pipeline management and scheduling, Pipedrive bundles both. Many teams use both for $24/seat/mo combined.

**Scheduling depth:** Calendly dominates. Round-robin distribution, collective scheduling (multiple hosts), routing forms that qualify leads before booking, buffer time, daily limits. Pipedrive's scheduler covers basics: share a link, prospect picks a time, activity gets created. No round-robin, no routing, no group events.

**CRM depth:** Pipedrive obviously wins. Visual pipelines, contact management, email tracking, sales forecasting, workflow automations. Calendly isn't a CRM and doesn't pretend to be. But its routing forms can function as lightweight lead qualification.

**The integration:** Calendly bookings sync to Pipedrive as activities, linked to existing contacts and deals. Many sales teams find this combo more effective than Pipedrive's built-in scheduler alone. Worth considering before you assume one tool handles everything.

## FAQ

**Can Pipedrive replace Calendly?** For basic booking links, yes. You'll miss round-robin, routing forms, group events, and the polished booking experience. Solo reps can make it work. Teams usually can't.

**Do they integrate?** Yes. Calendly's native Pipedrive integration syncs bookings as activities linked to contacts and deals. Works well.

**Which one should I get first?** If you don't have a CRM yet, probably Pipedrive. If you already have a CRM and just need better scheduling, Calendly.
