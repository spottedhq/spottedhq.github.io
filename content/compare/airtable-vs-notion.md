**Airtable** is for teams that need a real relational database with automations. It costs more but handles complex, interconnected data in ways Notion can't touch.

**Notion** is for teams that want docs, wikis, databases, and project management in one place. $10/user/mo gets you more features per dollar than almost anything else.

## The details

**Pricing:** The gap is significant. A 10-person team pays $200/mo on Airtable Team vs $100/mo on Notion Plus. Notion's Business plan ($20/user) includes AI and SSO. You'd need Airtable's $45/user tier for that.

**Data power:** Airtable wins. Linked records, rollups, lookups, formulas across tables. You can build data models that rival a real database. Notion databases work great for task lists and content calendars but struggle with deeply interconnected data.

**Documentation:** Notion wins. The block editor is the best in class for creating rich, structured documents. If your team needs a wiki or SOPs alongside project data, Notion is the obvious pick.

**Automations:** Airtable's engine is far more mature. Multi-step workflows, conditional logic, JavaScript scripting, Slack posts, email sends. Notion's automations are basic by comparison.

**Ease of use:** Notion has the gentler learning curve. Most teams get productive in a day. Airtable requires upfront planning around table structure and relationships, but the payoff is a more organized system long-term.

## FAQ

**Which has the better free plan?** Notion. Unlimited pages with no record limits. Airtable caps you at 1,000 records per base, which you can hit fast.

**Can I use both?** Yes. Many teams use Notion for docs and Airtable for data-heavy workflows. Zapier syncs data between them.

**Can I switch from Airtable to Notion?** You can export as CSV and import, but you'll lose relational links, automations, and custom views. Simple data migrates fine. Complex setups don't.
