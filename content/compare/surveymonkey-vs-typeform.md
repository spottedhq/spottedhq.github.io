**SurveyMonkey** is the research-grade survey tool. 250+ templates, advanced analytics with cross-tabs and trend tracking, and 1,000 responses/mo on the entry plan. Pick it when data analysis matters more than form design.

**Typeform** is the form builder people actually enjoy filling out. One question at a time, 2-3x higher completion rates, and it looks great embedded on your site. Pick it for lead gen, quizzes, and anything customer-facing.

## The details

**Pricing:** SurveyMonkey costs more at the door ($39 vs $25), but you get 1,000 responses vs Typeform's 100. At real business volumes, SurveyMonkey is better value per response. Typeform's 100-response Basic plan is tight for anything beyond hobby use.

**Analytics:** SurveyMonkey wins by a mile. Cross-tabulation, statistical significance testing, sentiment analysis, AI summaries, exportable charts. Typeform gives you completion rates and basic filtering. Most Typeform power users export to Google Sheets for real analysis.

**Respondent experience:** Typeform wins. The one-question-at-a-time format reduces cognitive load and keeps people engaged, especially on mobile. SurveyMonkey's wall-of-questions format works for structured research but has higher abandonment rates.

**Integrations:** SurveyMonkey goes deeper with enterprise tools (Salesforce, Power BI, Microsoft Teams). Typeform connects better with marketing tools (HubSpot, Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign). Both work with Zapier.

## FAQ

**Which has better completion rates?** Typeform, consistently. The conversational format keeps people engaged. It matters most for customer-facing forms and mobile users.

**Which is better for NPS?** SurveyMonkey. Dedicated NPS templates, built-in calculation, trend tracking, and industry benchmarking. Typeform can collect NPS scores but the analysis is basic.

**Can I accept payments?** Typeform yes (via Stripe). SurveyMonkey no.

**Is Google Forms good enough?** For basic surveys, yes. Free with unlimited responses. It lacks Typeform's design and SurveyMonkey's analytics, but it covers simple use cases at zero cost.
