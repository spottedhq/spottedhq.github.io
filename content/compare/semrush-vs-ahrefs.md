## The details

**Pricing:** The gap is huge at entry level. Ahrefs Starter at $29/mo vs Semrush Pro at $139.95/mo. At comparable feature levels (Ahrefs Lite $129/mo vs Semrush Pro $139.95/mo), they're close, but Semrush includes PPC and content tools. Watch for hidden costs on Ahrefs: extra users run $40-80/mo and daily rank tracking costs $50/mo extra.

**Backlinks:** Ahrefs wins. Largest index, most frequently updated, best link analysis tools. If backlink research is your primary use case, there's no better tool. Semrush's backlink data has improved but still trails in raw coverage.

**Content and PPC:** Semrush wins by a mile. Topic research, SEO writing assistant, content auditing, PPC research, social media scheduling. Ahrefs doesn't offer any of this. If you need those capabilities, you'll need separate tools alongside Ahrefs.

**Ease of use:** Ahrefs has a cleaner, less cluttered interface. New users find it easier to learn. Semrush packs more features into the dashboard, which means a steeper learning curve but less tool-switching once you know your way around.

**Keyword research:** Both are excellent. Semrush has a slightly larger keyword database and includes PPC metrics. Ahrefs offers click data that accounts for zero-click searches, giving a more realistic traffic picture.

## FAQ

**Is Semrush worth $139.95/mo?** If you use the full feature set (SEO + PPC + content + social), yes. It replaces multiple subscriptions. If you only need SEO, Ahrefs is more cost-effective.

**Can I try Ahrefs for free?** Webmaster Tools is free but limited to your own verified site. No free trial of paid plans. The $29/mo Starter plan is the cheapest way in.

**Which has better keyword data?** Both are top-tier. Semrush has a larger database with PPC metrics. Ahrefs has click data for more realistic traffic estimates. For most SEO work, either is sufficient.
