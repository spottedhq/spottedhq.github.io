**Writesonic** is an SEO content platform disguised as an AI writer. It generates long-form articles with real-time research, competitor analysis, and optimization for both Google and AI search engines. Starts at $39/mo.

**Rytr** is a budget AI writing assistant for quick short-form copy. Emails, ads, social posts, product descriptions. 40+ templates, dead simple to use. Unlimited words for $29/mo.

You publish blog content regularly and need it optimized for search. You want 1,000-5,000 word articles generated with real-time research and fact-checking. You care about AI search visibility (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini). You're comfortable spending $39+/mo for a professional content tool.

## Pick Rytr if...

You need quick ad copy, emails, and social posts on a budget. You're a solopreneur or freelancer who writes occasionally. You want a tool you can learn in 5 minutes. You don't need SEO optimization or long-form content. $9/mo or $29/mo unlimited is your speed.

## The details

**Pricing:** Rytr's Unlimited plan ($29/mo) costs less than Writesonic's cheapest paid plan ($39/mo). If budget is the main concern, Rytr wins. Annual billing drops Rytr's Saver to about $7/mo, which is hard to argue with.

**Content quality:** Different leagues. Writesonic's Article Writer 6.0 researches your topic, checks what's ranking, structures articles with proper headings, and adds internal links. Rytr generates decent short-form copy but anything over 500 words needs heavy editing and manual stitching.

**SEO tools:** Writesonic has a built-in SEO checker, keyword optimizer, meta tag generator, and a GEO tool that tracks your content across AI search platforms. Rytr has SEO meta description templates and that's about it.

**Ease of use:** Rytr wins. Pick a template, choose a tone, enter a brief, click generate. Zero learning curve. Writesonic has more features and more to learn. The Article Writer workflow involves topic selection, outline review, and SEO settings. Worth it if you need the output quality, but not a 5-minute tool.

**Plagiarism checking:** Both include it. Rytr's Copyscape integration is available even on the free plan, which is generous for the price.

## FAQ

**Can Rytr write long blog posts?** Technically yes, but you'll generate sections separately and stitch them together. No SEO optimization, no fact-checking, no real-time research. For serious blog content, Writesonic is the better tool.

**Is the Rytr free plan useful?** Yes. 10,000 characters/mo is roughly 1,500-2,000 words. Enough for a handful of emails or social posts. Good for testing before you pay.

**Does Writesonic have a free plan?** Yes, with limited access to GPT-4o mini and Claude Haiku. Enough to test the interface, not enough for real production use.
