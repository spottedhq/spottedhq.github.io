## What Is Teachable?

Teachable is an online course platform that has helped over 100,000 creators sell digital courses, coaching sessions, and memberships. Founded in 2014, it has become one of the most recognizable names in the creator economy. Teachable handles everything from course hosting and payment processing to student management and sales pages.

What sets Teachable apart is its focus on the business side of course creation. The platform includes built-in sales funnels, one-click upsells, abandoned checkout recovery, and a native affiliate marketing program. If you think of yourself as a course entrepreneur more than a pure educator, Teachable speaks your language.

In 2026, Teachable restructured its pricing into four tiers: Starter, Builder, Growth, and Advanced. The old Basic/Pro/Pro+ naming is gone. The biggest change is that the free plan was discontinued entirely -- you now start with a 7-day free trial on any paid plan. Teachable uses its own payment processor (Teachable:pay) which simplifies tax handling and payouts but gives you less control than processing through your own Stripe account.

## What Is Thinkific?

Thinkific is a Canadian-based online course platform that competes head-to-head with Teachable. It powers over 50,000 course creators and has a reputation for being the more educator-friendly option, with deeper course customization and a flexible content builder.

Thinkific's standout feature is its course builder, which natively supports a wider range of content types than Teachable -- including surveys, assignments, multimedia lessons, and a video library that lets you reuse uploaded content across multiple courses without re-uploading. This makes it especially appealing if you plan to build a large course catalog.

In 2026, Thinkific offers four plans: Basic ($36/mo annual), Start ($74/mo), Grow ($149/mo), and Thinkific Plus (enterprise). Like Teachable, Thinkific also killed its free plan and now offers a 14-day free trial instead. One major advantage Thinkific has always held: zero transaction fees on every plan. Your payment processor (Stripe or PayPal) takes their standard cut, but Thinkific itself never touches your revenue.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

But there is a catch. Teachable's Starter plan charges a **7.5% transaction fee** on every sale. If you sell $2,000 worth of courses per month, that is $150 in fees on top of your $29 subscription -- effectively making it more expensive than Thinkific's $36 Basic plan with zero transaction fees. The transaction fee disappears on Teachable's Builder plan ($69/mo) and above.

Both platforms offer annual billing discounts of roughly 20-25%. Neither offers a free plan anymore.

### Features

**Course building:** Thinkific wins here. Its drag-and-drop builder supports more content types natively, including surveys, assignments, and a reusable video library. Teachable's course editor is clean and straightforward but more limited -- you will need external tools like Google Forms for surveys.

**Marketing and sales:** Teachable wins decisively. Built-in sales funnels, one-click upsells, abandoned checkout recovery, customizable checkout pages, and native affiliate management give Teachable a clear edge for creators who focus on conversion optimization.

**Community features:** Thinkific offers built-in online communities where students can interact, plus live events and digital downloads. Teachable has added community features but they feel less mature.

**Integrations:** Thinkific has an App Store with 90+ integrations including ActiveCampaign, Mailchimp, Shopify, and Salesforce. Teachable integrates well with major tools through Zapier and has a solid API on the Advanced plan.

**Certificates and compliance:** Both platforms offer course completion certificates. Thinkific includes them from the Start plan, while Teachable offers them from Builder onward.

### Ease of Use

Teachable has a slight edge in day-to-day usability. Its interface is more intuitive, and you can alter lesson settings without drilling into your curriculum tree. The onboarding flow walks you through creating your first course quickly.

Thinkific has a steeper initial learning curve but rewards you with more customization options once you are comfortable. The theme editor gives you more control over your school's appearance, and the site builder is more flexible than Teachable's.

For complete beginners, Teachable is easier to start with. For creators who want more control and do not mind a learning curve, Thinkific is more powerful.

### Support

Thinkific has a G2 award-winning support team and offers live chat on paid plans. Teachable provides email support on Starter and adds live chat from Builder onward. Both have extensive knowledge bases and active communities.

## Who Should Use What?

**Pick Teachable if:** you are a solo creator or entrepreneur who wants to sell courses with minimal friction. You value built-in marketing tools like upsells, abandoned cart recovery, and affiliate programs. You want a simple, polished interface and do not need heavy course customization.

**Pick Thinkific if:** you are an educator or organization building a comprehensive course catalog. You want zero transaction fees from day one, a more flexible course builder, and built-in community features. You plan to offer diverse content types like assignments, surveys, and live events.

## FAQ

### Is Teachable better than Thinkific?

It depends on your priorities. Teachable is better for marketing and sales optimization -- its built-in funnels and upsell tools are superior. Thinkific is better for course creation flexibility and charges zero transaction fees on all plans, which can save you hundreds per month at scale.

### Can I switch from Teachable to Thinkific?

Yes, but it requires manual work. Neither platform offers a one-click migration tool. You will need to export your course content and student data, then rebuild your courses on the new platform. Student accounts and progress do not transfer automatically, so plan the migration during a quiet period.

### Do either platform take a cut of my sales?

Thinkific never takes a percentage of your sales. Teachable charges 7.5% on the Starter plan but drops to 0% from the Builder plan ($69/mo) onward. Both platforms process payments through their own systems, with standard credit card processing fees applying separately.

### Which platform is better for coaching?

Teachable has stronger coaching features, including one-on-one coaching products and milestone tracking. Thinkific supports coaching through live events and community features but does not have a dedicated coaching product type.

If you are building a larger course library or prioritize the teaching experience over the selling experience, **Thinkific's Basic plan at $36/mo** gives you unlimited courses with no transaction fees at a lower entry price.

Start with whichever aligns with your priorities and take advantage of the free trials before committing.
