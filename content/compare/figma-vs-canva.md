**Figma** is a professional UI/UX design tool for teams shipping apps and websites. If you need prototyping, component systems, and developer handoff, this is the industry standard.

**Canva** is for everyone who needs good-looking visuals without design skills. Social posts, presentations, video thumbnails, print materials. Millions of templates, drag and drop, done.

## The details

**Pricing:** Canva is dramatically cheaper for teams. Five people on Canva Teams costs $14.99/mo total. Five Full Seats on Figma Professional costs ~$80/mo. But these tools don't compete directly. If you need Figma's capabilities, Canva isn't a substitute at any price.

**What they actually do:** Figma gives you vector editing on an infinite canvas, auto-layout, component variants, prototyping, and code export. Canva gives you template-first design with drag-and-drop, stock photos, video editing, AI background removal, and even print-on-demand. They solve different problems.

**Ease of use:** Canva wins and it's not close. Someone with zero design experience can produce a professional graphic in minutes. Figma has a real learning curve. Components, auto-layout, and constraints take weeks to master. It's a professional tool that rewards investment.

**AI features:** Canva's are more practical today. Background removal, image generation, Magic Eraser, Magic Write. Figma's AI focuses on layout suggestions and design assistance. For content creation, Canva's AI toolkit is more useful right now.

**The real answer:** Most companies in 2026 use both. Designers build the brand system in Figma. Everyone else creates on-brand content in Canva.

## FAQ

**Can Canva replace Figma for UI design?** No. Canva lacks prototyping, component systems, auto-layout, and developer handoff. It can make static mockups that look like app screens, but it can't produce developer-ready deliverables.

**Can Figma replace Canva for marketing graphics?** Technically yes, practically no. Making a social post in Figma takes 5x longer because there are no marketing templates or stock libraries.

**Is Figma free enough for solo designers?** The 3-file limit is tight. Most solo designers end up on Professional at $16/mo. Students and educators get full access free.
