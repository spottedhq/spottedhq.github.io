## The details

**Pricing:** Cal.com's free plan is the story here. Unlimited event types and bookings vs Calendly's single event type. For solo freelancers and consultants, that's a complete scheduling solution at $0. For teams, pricing is close: Calendly Teams at $16/seat vs Cal.com Teams at $15/user.

**Polish:** Calendly's booking pages are more refined. They load fast, look professional, and the UX has been honed over years. Cal.com's pages are functional and clean but slightly less polished. Cal.com branding shows on the free plan.

**Integrations:** Calendly has the bigger library. Native Salesforce, HubSpot, PayPal, Stripe, and hundreds more. Cal.com covers Zoom, Google Meet, Stripe, and Zapier, but the selection is smaller. Check Cal.com's integration page before committing if you need a specific CRM connector.

**Customization:** Cal.com wins for technical teams. Open-source means you can modify anything: custom booking flows, API-driven scheduling, webhooks, self-hosted deployments with full data control. Calendly limits you to what their admin panel offers.

**Compliance:** Cal.com Organizations includes SOC 2 and HIPAA compliance, plus self-hosting for full data control. Calendly Enterprise offers SSO and SCIM but no self-hosting option.

## FAQ

**Is Cal.com really free?** Yes, for individual users. Unlimited event types and bookings. The limits: one user, Cal.com branding, no team features.

**Can Cal.com replace Calendly?** For most use cases, yes. The gaps are in CRM integrations (Calendly's Salesforce connector is more mature) and enterprise admin features.

**Can I self-host Cal.com?** Yes. MIT license, deploy with Docker or Vercel. Free regardless of team size, but you handle maintenance and uptime.
