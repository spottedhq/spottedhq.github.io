## What Is Calendly?

Calendly is the scheduling tool most people think of first. You create event types (15-minute call, 30-minute demo, etc.), share your booking link, and people pick a time that works. It syncs with your calendar, avoids double-bookings, sends automatic reminders, and handles timezone conversions. Simple, effective, and used by over 20 million people.

What makes Calendly sticky is its reliability and polish. The booking experience for invitees is clean and professional. Setup takes about 10 minutes. Calendar sync works flawlessly with Google Calendar, Outlook, and iCloud. And the integration ecosystem is mature. Calendly connects natively with HubSpot, Salesforce, PayPal, Stripe, Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams, plus hundreds of tools via Zapier.

For teams, Calendly adds round-robin scheduling (distribute meetings across team members), collective events (find a time when multiple hosts are free), and lead routing (send inbound bookings to the right person based on rules). The Teams plan includes Salesforce integration and admin controls for managing team-wide scheduling. Enterprise adds SSO, SCIM, and dedicated support.

Calendly's main drawback is its pricing model. The free plan is limited to a single event type, which pushes most users to the Standard plan quickly. And at $10-16/seat/mo, costs scale linearly with team size. A 20-person team pays $200-320/mo for what is fundamentally a calendar booking page.

## What Is Cal.com?

Cal.com is an open-source scheduling platform that launched as a direct alternative to Calendly. The pitch is straightforward: everything Calendly does, but open source, self-hostable, and with a free plan that doesn't artificially limit you.

The free plan is Cal.com's strongest selling point. You get unlimited event types, unlimited bookings, and full calendar integrations, all for free. The catch? You're limited to one user, and Cal.com branding appears on your booking pages. For solo founders, freelancers, and consultants, this is a complete scheduling solution at zero cost.

Being open source (MIT license) means you can self-host Cal.com on your own infrastructure. This matters for companies with data residency requirements, strict privacy policies, or developers who want to customize the scheduling logic beyond what any SaaS tool allows. The codebase is on GitHub with an active community and regular updates.

Paid plans kick in when you need teams, custom branding, or advanced features. The Teams plan ($15/user/mo) adds shared event types, round-robin scheduling, and team workflows. The Organizations plan ($37/user/mo) adds SSO, SCIM provisioning, SOC 2 compliance, and enterprise-grade admin controls. Both come with a 14-day free trial.

Cal.com is newer than Calendly and still catching up in some areas: the integration library is smaller, the booking page design is less polished (though functional), and enterprise features like advanced analytics are less mature. But the gap has narrowed significantly through 2025-2026.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Pricing

This is where Cal.com has a structural advantage.

**Calendly pricing (per seat/month):**
- **Free:** 1 event type, 1 calendar connection, basic integrations
- **Standard:** $10/seat/mo (annual) or $12/seat/mo (monthly) — unlimited event types, multiple calendars, group events, HubSpot/PayPal/Stripe integrations
- **Teams:** $16/seat/mo (annual) or $20/seat/mo (monthly) — Salesforce, round-robin, lead routing, admin controls. Volume discounts kick in above 30 seats.
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing (minimum ~$15,000/year) — SSO, SCIM, dedicated support

**Cal.com pricing (per user/month):**
- **Free:** Unlimited event types, unlimited bookings, calendar integrations, 1 user, Cal.com branding
- **Teams:** $15/user/mo — team scheduling, round-robin, custom branding, 14-day free trial
- **Organizations:** $37/user/mo — SSO, SCIM, SOC 2/HIPAA compliance, advanced admin
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing
- **Self-hosted:** Free (you manage infrastructure)

For a single user, Cal.com's free plan crushes Calendly's. Unlimited event types vs. one event type. It's not even close. A freelancer or consultant can run their entire scheduling operation on Cal.com for $0.

For teams, the pricing is comparable. Calendly Teams at $16/seat/mo vs. Cal.com Teams at $15/user/mo. But Cal.com's self-hosting option (free for any team size, you just run the infrastructure) is a wildcard that Calendly can't match.

### Features

**Booking experience:** Calendly's booking pages are more polished. They load fast, look professional, and the UX has been refined over years. Cal.com's booking pages are functional and clean but slightly less polished. Cal.com branding on the free plan is the main visual difference.

**Event types and scheduling logic:** Both support one-on-one, group, round-robin, and collective scheduling. Calendly's routing logic is more mature, with better support for conditional routing based on form answers. Cal.com covers the basics well and is improving rapidly.

**Calendar integrations:** Both sync with Google Calendar, Outlook, and Apple Calendar. Calendly's calendar sync is rock-solid and handles edge cases (recurring events, all-day blockers) reliably. Cal.com's sync is good but occasionally less graceful with complex calendar setups.

**Third-party integrations:** Calendly has the larger integration library, with native connections to Salesforce, HubSpot, PayPal, Stripe, Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, and hundreds more via Zapier. Cal.com integrates with Zoom, Google Meet, Stripe, Zapier, and a growing list of tools, but the library is smaller. If you need a specific CRM or payment integration, check Cal.com's integration page before committing.

**Customization:** Cal.com wins for technical users. Open-source means you can modify anything: custom booking flows, API-driven scheduling, webhooks, and self-hosted deployments with full data control. Calendly's customization is limited to what their admin panel offers (branding, colors, form fields).

**Payments:** Both integrate with Stripe for paid bookings. Calendly also supports PayPal natively. For coaches, consultants, and freelancers who charge for meetings, both tools handle this well.

**Compliance and security:** Cal.com Organizations includes SOC 2 and HIPAA compliance features, plus the option to self-host for full data control. Calendly Enterprise offers SSO, SCIM, and security certifications, but no self-hosting option. For highly regulated industries, Cal.com's self-hosting gives it an edge.

### Ease of Use

Calendly is easier for non-technical users. The onboarding flow walks you through creating your first event type in under 5 minutes. Everything works out of the box. Your booking link is immediately shareable, and calendar sync just happens.

Cal.com's cloud version is nearly as easy to set up for basic use. Creating event types, connecting calendars, and sharing links is straightforward. Where it gets more complex is if you self-host (you need to manage deployment, updates, and infrastructure) or if you want to customize beyond the default options.

For teams, Calendly's admin dashboard is more intuitive. Managing team members, permissions, and scheduling rules is point-and-click. Cal.com's team management works but feels more developer-oriented.

## FAQ

### Is Cal.com really free?

Yes, for individual users. The free plan includes unlimited event types, unlimited bookings, and calendar integrations. The limitations are: one user only, Cal.com branding on booking pages, and no team features. For teams, you'll need the $15/user/mo Teams plan.

### Can Cal.com replace Calendly?

For most use cases, yes. Cal.com covers one-on-one scheduling, group events, round-robin, payment collection, and calendar sync. The gaps are in CRM integrations (Calendly's Salesforce integration is more mature), booking page polish, and enterprise admin features. If you rely heavily on Calendly's Salesforce connector, test Cal.com's integration thoroughly before switching.

### Can I self-host Cal.com?

Yes. Cal.com is open source under the MIT license. You can deploy it on your own servers using Docker, Vercel, or any hosting platform that runs Node.js. Self-hosting is free regardless of team size, but you're responsible for maintenance, updates, and uptime.

### Does Calendly have a free plan?

Yes, but it's limited to one event type and one calendar connection. For anything beyond basic scheduling, you'll need the Standard plan at $10/seat/mo. Cal.com's free plan is significantly more generous.

### Can I migrate from Calendly to Cal.com?

There's no automated migration tool. You'll need to recreate your event types and update your booking links. Calendar integrations reconnect quickly. The main friction is updating any existing booking links you've shared, since old Calendly links won't redirect to Cal.com.
