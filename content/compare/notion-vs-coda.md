**Notion** is the polished all-in-one workspace for docs, wikis, and project management. Block-based editor, great templates, and AI built into the Business plan. It does 80% of everything well.

**Coda** is the doc that acts like an app. Deeper formulas, stronger automation, 600+ integrations via Packs. It's more powerful but steeper to learn. You only pay for doc makers; viewers are free.

| | **Notion** | **Coda** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $10/user/mo (Plus) | $10/doc maker/mo (Pro) |
| **Free tier** | Yes (individual, 5MB uploads) | Yes (unlimited editors, limited doc size) |
| **Best for** | All-in-one workspace, team wikis | Custom workflows, doc-based apps |
| **Standout feature** | Native AI (summarize, Q&A, autofill) | Formula language + automation engine |
| **Integrations** | 70+ native | 600+ via Packs |
| **Billing model** | Per user | Per doc maker (viewers free) |

## Pick Notion if...

You want one tool for docs, wikis, project management, and light databases. You value polish, a huge template ecosystem, and integrated AI. Your team mostly creates and organizes content.

You have power users who want to build custom workflows, interactive dashboards, or doc-based apps. You need deep integrations with external tools. Your team has many viewers but few builders (the doc maker billing model saves real money).

## The details

**Pricing:** Both start at $10/mo, but they bill differently. Notion charges every user. Coda charges only doc makers. A team of 20 where 5 build docs and 15 view them costs $200/mo on Notion Plus but $50/mo on Coda Pro. If everyone creates content, Notion's per-user pricing is more predictable.

**Power vs polish:** Notion's learning curve is gentle. You'll create useful pages in minutes. Coda's formula language is closer to a real programming language. It takes investment to master, but you can build things in Coda that aren't possible in Notion.

**AI:** Notion wins here. Notion AI is natively built in, searches across your workspace, and handles summarization, writing, and Q&A. Coda has some AI capabilities through Packs, but it's not as deeply integrated.

**The honest take:** Most teams should start with Notion. It covers more ground with less friction. Switch to Coda only if you hit Notion's limits on formulas, automation, or integrations, and you have someone on the team willing to build with it.

## FAQ

**Can Notion replace Coda?** For docs, wikis, and project management, yes. For complex automations and custom app-like docs, no.

**Which is better for solo users?** Notion. The free plan is generous, the learning curve is manageable, and you won't need Coda's power features.

**Can I migrate between them?** Coda has an official Notion importer. Going the other direction requires more manual work.
