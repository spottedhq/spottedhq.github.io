Choosing between **HubSpot** and **Brevo**? Here's the short version: **HubSpot** is the all-in-one platform for growing businesses that want CRM, marketing, sales, and service under one roof -- but it gets expensive fast. **Brevo** is the budget-friendly email and marketing platform that punches well above its price point, especially for small businesses that need email, SMS, and WhatsApp marketing without breaking the bank. HubSpot is the ecosystem; Brevo is the focused tool.

## What Is HubSpot?

HubSpot is the 800-pound gorilla of the CRM and marketing world. Founded in 2006 as an inbound marketing company, it has expanded into a full business platform with five "Hubs" -- Marketing, Sales, Service, Content, and Operations -- all built on top of a free CRM. For businesses that buy into the ecosystem, everything connects: your contacts, deals, emails, support tickets, and analytics all live in one place.

The **free CRM** is genuinely useful. You get up to 1,000 contacts, 2,000 email sends per month, one deal pipeline, basic reporting (3 dashboards, 10 reports each), live chat with HubSpot branding, up to 20 landing pages, and access for up to 2 users. Many small businesses run on the free plan for months before upgrading.

Where HubSpot gets complicated -- and expensive -- is the paid tiers. **Marketing Hub Starter** at $20/month includes 1,000 marketing contacts, removes HubSpot branding from emails, adds 10 automated email actions, and provides 25 active lists. **Professional** jumps to $800/month (annual commitment required, 3 seats included) and unlocks the features most marketers actually want: full marketing automation, A/B testing, social media management, custom reporting, lead scoring, and omnichannel campaign tools. **Enterprise** at $3,600/month (5 seats included) adds advanced permissions, custom objects, predictive lead scoring, and a mandatory $7,000 onboarding fee.

The pricing math can surprise you. HubSpot charges based on marketing contacts -- any contact you want to market to. Exceeding your contact tier triggers automatic upgrades. Add multiple Hubs (Sales, Service, etc.) and the costs compound. A growing business can easily spend $1,000-2,000/month before realizing it.

## What Is Brevo?

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) is a marketing platform that was built with a radically different pricing philosophy: charge based on emails sent, not contacts stored. This single decision makes Brevo dramatically cheaper for most small businesses, because you can store unlimited contacts on every plan and only pay for the volume of messages you actually send.

The **Free plan** is generous in ways that matter: unlimited contact storage, 300 emails per day (roughly 9,000 per month), a drag-and-drop editor, email templates, campaign analytics, segmentation, basic CRM, and limited automation access. The main constraint is the daily sending limit and Brevo branding on emails.

**Starter** ($9/month for 5,000 emails) removes the daily cap and adds basic analytics. **Standard** ($18/month) is where Brevo gets interesting: you get landing pages, multi-user access, priority email support, and A/B testing. **Professional** ($499/month) is for high-volume senders with advanced needs. All prices scale with email volume -- you can adjust your plan to match exactly what you send.

What makes Brevo stand out from most email platforms is the **multichannel approach**. SMS marketing and WhatsApp campaigns are built into the platform, not bolted on as expensive add-ons. For businesses that communicate with customers across email, text, and messaging apps, this is a genuine differentiator. The platform also includes transactional email (order confirmations, password resets) on the same system, eliminating the need for a separate provider.

There are some hidden costs to be aware of. Removing the Brevo logo costs an extra $10.80/month. Sales features (Sales Essentials or Sales Advanced) run $27.92-$58.50 per user per month. These add-ons can change the value equation if you need them.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Pricing

Brevo is dramatically cheaper at every comparable tier. HubSpot Marketing Hub Professional at $800/month costs more than 44x Brevo Standard at $18/month. Even HubSpot Starter at $20/month is double Brevo Starter at $9/month.

The pricing models are fundamentally different, which makes direct comparison tricky:

| | HubSpot | Brevo |
|---|---|---|
| Free plan | 1,000 contacts, 2,000 emails/mo | Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day |
| Entry paid | $20/mo (1K contacts) | $9/mo (5K emails) |
| Full features | $800/mo (Professional) | $18/mo (Standard) |
| Enterprise | $3,600/mo + $7K onboarding | $499/mo |

HubSpot charges per marketing contact -- if your list grows, your bill grows automatically. Brevo charges per email sent -- store as many contacts as you want, pay only when you message them. For businesses with large lists but moderate sending frequency, Brevo can be 10-50x cheaper.

### Features

**HubSpot wins on:** CRM depth (deal pipelines, contact scoring, lifecycle stages), marketing automation sophistication (complex workflows with branching logic), reporting and analytics (custom dashboards, attribution modeling), sales tools (sequences, meeting scheduling, playbooks), content management (blog, landing pages, SEO), and the all-in-one platform advantage where everything connects natively.

**Brevo wins on:** price-to-feature ratio (Standard at $18/month includes A/B testing that costs $800/month on HubSpot), multichannel messaging (email + SMS + WhatsApp in one platform), transactional email (built-in, not a separate product), contact storage (unlimited on all plans), and simplicity (fewer features means less complexity to manage).

### Ease of Use

Brevo is simpler to get started with. The email editor is clean, the automation builder is visual and straightforward, and there are fewer concepts to learn. Most users can send their first campaign within 30 minutes of signing up.

HubSpot has a steeper learning curve because the platform does so much more. The CRM alone has contact properties, lifecycle stages, deal pipelines, and lead scoring to configure. Marketing workflows can be complex. The upside is that HubSpot invests heavily in education -- HubSpot Academy offers free certifications and the documentation is excellent. But expect to spend days, not hours, getting fully set up.

## Who Should Use What?

**Pick HubSpot if:** you want a single platform for marketing, sales, and customer service. You are a growing business (10+ employees) that needs sophisticated CRM and automation. You value deep analytics and attribution modeling. You are willing to invest $800+/month for a professional-grade marketing stack. You want a platform that scales from startup to enterprise without migration.

**Pick Brevo if:** you are a small business or solopreneur that needs email marketing, SMS, and basic CRM without enterprise pricing. Your contact list is large but your sending volume is moderate. You need transactional emails alongside marketing campaigns. You communicate with customers via SMS or WhatsApp in addition to email. Budget is a primary concern and you want maximum capability per dollar spent.

## FAQ

### Is the HubSpot free CRM really free forever?

Yes, with limitations. The free CRM includes up to 1,000 contacts, 2,000 email sends per month, basic reporting, and HubSpot branding on all customer-facing features (chat widgets, email footers, forms). There are no time limits -- you can use it indefinitely. The limitations are designed to make you want to upgrade as your business grows, which is exactly what happens for most users.

### Can Brevo replace HubSpot?

For email marketing and basic CRM, yes. For advanced CRM, sales pipeline management, content management, and enterprise reporting, no. Brevo is a marketing communication tool; HubSpot is a business operating system. If you only use HubSpot for email campaigns, switching to Brevo could save you hundreds per month.

### Does Brevo include automation?

Yes, even on the free plan (limited). The Standard plan at $18/month includes visual automation workflows, A/B testing, and segmentation. The automation builder is simpler than HubSpot's but covers the needs of most small businesses: welcome series, abandoned cart emails, lead nurturing sequences, and re-engagement campaigns.

### How does HubSpot's contact pricing work?

HubSpot charges based on "marketing contacts" -- contacts you actively market to. Non-marketing contacts (stored but not emailed) are free up to 15 million. If your marketing contact count exceeds your tier, HubSpot automatically upgrades you to the next pricing level. This can lead to unexpected bill increases as your list grows.

### Can I use both together?

Yes. Some businesses use HubSpot as their CRM and Brevo for email delivery (Brevo's transactional email API and lower per-email costs). Brevo integrates with HubSpot natively, so contacts sync between systems.

## Our Recommendation

For small businesses and solopreneurs, **Brevo Standard** at $18/month is an exceptional value. You get email marketing with A/B testing, SMS and WhatsApp campaigns, landing pages, automation, and unlimited contact storage. No other platform offers this feature set at this price point. Start with the free plan to test the waters -- 300 emails per day is enough for most early-stage businesses.

For growing businesses that need a unified CRM and marketing platform, **HubSpot** is worth the investment -- but be strategic about it. Start with the **free CRM** to learn the ecosystem, upgrade to **Starter** ($20/month) when you need to remove branding and add automation, and only jump to **Professional** ($800/month) when you have the revenue and team size to justify it. The Professional tier is where HubSpot's real power lives, but it is priced for businesses generating meaningful revenue.

The honest advice: if you are spending more on HubSpot than you are generating from it, switch to Brevo and reinvest the savings into your actual marketing campaigns.
