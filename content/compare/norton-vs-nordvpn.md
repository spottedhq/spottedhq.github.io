Choosing between **Norton** and **NordVPN**? Here's the short version: these products serve different purposes. **NordVPN** is a dedicated VPN, and it shows: faster speeds, more servers, better streaming, stronger privacy features. **Norton 360** is a security suite that includes antivirus, a password manager, dark web monitoring, and a VPN bundled in. If you need a great VPN, pick NordVPN. If you need a complete security suite and want a decent VPN included, Norton 360 covers more ground in one subscription.

## What Is Norton 360?

Norton 360 is a full cybersecurity suite from Gen Digital (formerly NortonLifeLock), combining antivirus, a VPN, a password manager, dark web monitoring, cloud backup, and identity theft protection into one subscription.

The antivirus is Norton's core strength, consistently scoring 99.9%+ detection rates in independent testing by AV-TEST and AV-Comparatives. Real-time threat protection, a smart firewall, and exploit prevention make it one of the most reliable endpoint security products available.

Norton's VPN (Norton Secure VPN) is included with all 360 plans, offering 2,000+ servers in 65 countries with AES-256 encryption and a kill switch. It handles basic privacy needs well. Pricing starts at $3.33/month for the first year (Standard, 1 device), but renewal prices typically double.

## What Is NordVPN?

NordVPN is a dedicated VPN service used by over 14 million people. It does one thing, protect your internet connection and privacy, and does it exceptionally well. Operating 7,400+ servers across 118 countries, NordVPN delivers some of the fastest speeds in the industry through its proprietary NordLynx protocol.

NordVPN's security features run deep: RAM-only servers, post-quantum encryption, Threat Protection (malware/tracker blocking), Meshnet (encrypted P2P connections), Double VPN, and obfuscated servers for bypassing VPN detection.

NordVPN's subscription tiers (Basic, Plus, Complete, Prime) add layers of functionality, from VPN-only up to identity theft insurance. The Plus tier adds Threat Protection Pro and NordPass (password manager), while Complete adds encrypted cloud storage. This tiered approach lets you build up to a security suite comparable to Norton.

### VPN Performance

This is not close. NordVPN is the significantly better VPN.

**Speed:** NordVPN averages ~780 Mbps vs Norton's ~400 Mbps. Roughly twice as fast.

**Server network:** NordVPN has 7,400+ servers in 118 countries vs Norton's 2,000+ in 65 countries.

**Protocols:** NordVPN uses NordLynx with post-quantum encryption. Norton uses WireGuard and IKEv2 with standard encryption.

**Advanced features:** NordVPN offers Double VPN, obfuscated servers, dedicated IP, Meshnet, and SmartDNS. Norton's VPN is basic. Connect and protect, nothing more.

### Streaming

**NordVPN** reliably unblocks Netflix (15+ regional libraries), Disney+, BBC iPlayer, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video, and most major streaming services. SmartDNS extends streaming to devices that cannot run VPN apps.

**Norton Secure VPN** can unblock some streaming services, including US Netflix and a few others, but is less reliable and covers fewer platforms. It is not a streaming-focused VPN and lacks features like SmartDNS.

### Security Beyond VPN

This is where Norton has an advantage, but only if you need what it offers.

**Norton 360 includes:** industry-leading antivirus, real-time malware protection, smart firewall, exploit prevention, password manager, dark web monitoring, cloud backup (up to 250GB on Premium), parental controls, and identity theft protection (higher tiers).

**NordVPN includes (with Plus or higher):** Threat Protection Pro (malware/tracker blocking at network level, not endpoint), NordPass (password manager), NordLocker (encrypted storage), dark web monitor, and identity theft insurance (Prime tier).

Norton's endpoint security is better here. It catches threats at the device level that a network-level blocker can't. If you don't have antivirus software, Norton 360 fills a real gap. If you already use Windows Defender, this advantage matters less.

### Privacy

**NordVPN** is the more privacy-focused product. Independent audits confirm its no-logs policy, RAM-only servers prevent data persistence, and Panama jurisdiction means no mandatory data retention.

**Norton** is a security company, not a privacy company. The VPN has a no-logs policy, but Norton's other products collect telemetry and usage data. If privacy is your primary concern, NordVPN is the stronger choice.

## Who Should Use What?

**Pick Norton 360 if:** you need a full security suite covering antivirus, VPN, password management, and identity protection in one subscription. It's the right choice for non-technical users who want one product that handles all their cybersecurity needs, or for families who want parental controls bundled in. The VPN is good enough for basic privacy (public Wi-Fi protection, IP masking) even if it isn't the best standalone VPN.

**Pick NordVPN if:** you want a high-performance VPN for privacy, streaming, torrenting, or bypassing geo-restrictions. NordVPN is the better choice for anyone who already has antivirus coverage (including Windows Defender) and wants the best VPN experience available. Pair it with the Plus or Complete tier if you want password management and encrypted storage.

## FAQ

### Can Norton's VPN replace NordVPN?

For basic use like encrypting public Wi-Fi and hiding your IP, yes. For streaming, speed-sensitive tasks, advanced features, or privacy-focused use, no. Norton's VPN is a solid add-on to a security suite, but it's not a competitive standalone VPN.

### Do I need antivirus if I have NordVPN?

A VPN and antivirus protect against different threats. A VPN encrypts your connection and hides your IP. Antivirus detects and removes malware on your device. NordVPN's Threat Protection blocks some threats at the network level, but it's not a replacement for endpoint antivirus. Windows Defender (included with Windows) covers most users adequately, but Norton provides stronger protection.

### Does Norton's price go up after the first year?

Yes, significantly. Norton's promotional first-year pricing typically doubles at renewal. Always check renewal pricing before subscribing. NordVPN's multi-year plans maintain consistent pricing for the commitment period.
