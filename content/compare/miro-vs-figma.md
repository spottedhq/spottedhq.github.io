**Miro** is the best online whiteboard for brainstorming, workshops, and visual planning. 2,500+ templates, infinite canvas, built-in facilitation tools. If your work involves sticky notes and diagrams, this is your tool.

**Figma** is the industry-standard design tool for UI/UX, prototyping, and design systems. If you design interfaces, there's nothing better. FigJam covers basic whiteboarding but doesn't match Miro's depth.

| | **Miro** | **Figma** |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | $8/member/mo | $12/editor/mo |
| **Free tier** | 3 editable boards | 3 design files |
| **Best for** | PMs, strategists, workshops | UI/UX designers, product teams |
| **Standout feature** | Workshop facilitation (voting, timers, guided collab) | Interactive prototyping + design systems |

## Pick Miro if...

You run workshops, plan projects visually, or need a shared space for cross-functional teams to brainstorm. You're a PM, strategist, consultant, or agile coach. Your output is diagrams and plans, not pixel-perfect screens.

## Pick Figma if...

You design user interfaces, build prototypes, or maintain design systems. You're a UI/UX designer, and your output is screens, components, and interactive prototypes. Nothing else comes close for this work.

## The details

**Pricing:** Miro is cheaper per seat ($8 vs $12), but charges every member. Figma only charges editors; viewers are free. For a team with a few designers and many stakeholders, Figma can be cheaper overall.

**The overlap:** FigJam (Figma's whiteboard) competes with Miro on sticky notes and basic brainstorming. Miro competes with Figma on basic wireframing. Neither replaces the other's core strength. Many product teams use both.

**Facilitation:** Miro has voting, timers, icebreakers, and presentation mode built for running structured workshops. Figma has better version history, branching, and design review tools. Different collaboration needs.

**AI features:** Miro's AI clusters sticky notes, generates summaries, and creates diagrams from text. Figma's AI helps with layout suggestions and asset search. Miro's is more mature for ideation; Figma's focuses on design production.

## FAQ

**Can Figma replace Miro?** FigJam covers basic whiteboarding. For heavy workshop facilitation and diagramming, Miro is still stronger.

**Can Miro replace Figma?** No. You can't build production UI designs, interactive prototypes, or component libraries in Miro.

**Should I use both?** If you're a product team that runs discovery workshops and then designs interfaces, yes. It's a common and productive combo.
