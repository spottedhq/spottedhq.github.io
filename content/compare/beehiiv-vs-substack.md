**Substack** is for writers who want zero friction. Start publishing immediately, tap into a built-in audience network, and pay nothing until you charge readers (then 10% forever).

**Beehiiv** is for creators who treat their newsletter as a business. Flat monthly pricing, multiple monetization paths, and advanced growth tools that Substack doesn't offer.

## The details

**Pricing:** Substack looks free but the 10% scales forever. At $50K/mo in subscription revenue, Substack takes $5,000/mo. Beehiiv would cost roughly $99-$159/mo for that tier. The crossover point is around $500-$600/mo in subscription revenue.

**Monetization:** Beehiiv gives you options. Native ad network for sponsorships. Digital product sales with zero commission. Paid subscriptions keeping 100% of revenue. Substack only does paid subscriptions with a 10% cut.

**Growth tools:** Beehiiv's Boosts marketplace lets you pay other creators to recommend you (or earn money recommending others). The referral program lets readers unlock rewards for sharing. Substack relies on its Notes feed and recommendations between writers.

**Ease of use:** Substack wins. Sign up, write, publish. Beehiiv requires more setup: configuring segments, designing templates, building automations. It's not hard, but there's real onboarding time.

**The smart play:** Many creators start on Substack for the simplicity and network, then migrate to Beehiiv when revenue justifies the switch. Beehiiv has a direct Substack import tool that makes this painless.

## FAQ

**Does Substack's 10% ever go away?** No. No cap, no volume discount, no way to negotiate it down.

**Can I monetize a free newsletter on Beehiiv?** Yes. The native ad network lets you earn through sponsorships without charging readers. Substack doesn't offer this.

**Which is better for free newsletters?** Both work well. Substack has better organic discovery. Beehiiv lets you monetize through ads without ever charging readers.
