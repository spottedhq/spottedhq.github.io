## The details

**Pricing:** Copy.ai is cheaper at entry level ($29 vs $39/mo). But Writesonic has a real free plan that Copy.ai doesn't match. At mid-tier, they're solving different problems: Writesonic Standard at $99/mo gives you SEO content tools, Copy.ai Agents at $249/mo gives you team workflow automation.

**Long-form content:** Writesonic wins clearly. Article Writer 6.0 produces structured blog posts with proper headings, keyword integration, and logical flow. Copy.ai's chat interface can produce long-form content but requires more prompting and manual work.

**SEO:** Writesonic's biggest advantage. Built-in keyword research, competitor analysis, on-page scoring, site auditing, and AI visibility tracking. Copy.ai has no native SEO tools. If you care about organic search, you'd need Ahrefs or Semrush alongside Copy.ai.

**Workflow automation:** Copy.ai's biggest advantage. Build multi-step workflows that research prospects, generate personalized emails, and queue content for review. Writesonic's automation is limited to its article pipeline.

**These tools have diverged.** A solo blogger choosing Copy.ai would overpay for automation they don't need. A marketing team choosing Writesonic would miss the workflow automation that could save hours daily. Pick based on what you actually do.

## FAQ

**Which produces better long-form content?** Writesonic. Article Writer 6.0's guided process produces more polished, structured output than Copy.ai's chat-based approach.

**Do either replace a human writer?** No. Both are force multipliers. AI output still needs human review for accuracy, brand voice, and originality.
