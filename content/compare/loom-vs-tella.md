**Loom** is the industry standard for async video messaging. Record your screen, share a link, done. Built for team communication, now part of Atlassian with Jira/Confluence integration.

**Tella** is for creators who care how their videos look. Custom layouts, branded backgrounds, zoom effects, transitions, and 4K/60fps export. It looks like you spent an hour editing, but you recorded it in one take.

## The details

**Pricing:** Nearly identical for individuals. Loom Business is $12.50/mo, Tella Pro is $12/mo (both annual). The difference is in what you get. Loom gives you sharing, analytics, and team workspace features. Tella gives you production quality and editing tools. Tella Premium at $39/mo adds no export time limit, custom branding, and video analytics.

**Recording and editing:** Tella wins here. Custom layouts (switch between full screen, camera-only, side-by-side mid-recording), branded backgrounds, auto-zoom that follows your cursor, smooth transitions, and auto-subtitles. Loom's editing is trim, stitch, and basic branding. If you want polished output from Loom, you still need an external editor.

**Sharing and team features:** Loom wins. Instant link sharing, viewer engagement analytics, team workspaces, and integrations with Slack, Notion, and Atlassian. Tella is primarily a creator tool, not a team messaging platform.

**Platform:** Loom has desktop, Chrome extension, and mobile apps. Tella is desktop only. If you need to record on your phone, Loom is the only option.

**AI features:** Both offer filler word removal and transcription. Loom adds auto-generated titles, summaries, and chapters (great for skimming). Tella's AI focuses on editing cleanup.

## FAQ

**Is Loom better than Tella?** For team communication, yes. For video quality and production polish, Tella wins. They solve different problems.

**Does Tella have a free plan?** No. Just a 7-day trial. Loom's free plan with 5-minute recordings is more accessible for casual use.

**Can I use both?** Many people do. Loom for quick team updates, Tella when the video needs to look good. Not a bad setup.
