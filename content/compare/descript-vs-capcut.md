**Descript** is for anyone who edits spoken-word content. Import a video, get a transcript, edit the text, and the audio/video follows. For podcasters and interviewers, nothing else comes close.

**CapCut** is for social media creators who need polished short-form video fast. Free plan includes 1080p exports with no watermark. Trending templates, mobile editing, and one-tap effects.

## The details

**Pricing:** CapCut wins at every tier. The free plan alone is more capable than Descript's paid Hobbyist plan (1080p without watermarks vs 720p with). CapCut Pro at $9.99/mo undercuts Descript Creator at $24/mo by more than half. But Descript's value comes from features CapCut doesn't have.

**The transcript trick:** Descript's core innovation is editing media by editing text. Delete a sentence from the transcript, the audio and video disappear. Rearrange paragraphs, the timeline follows. For a 60-minute podcast, this saves hours compared to scrubbing a timeline. CapCut can't do this.

**Social media speed:** CapCut is built for it. Thousands of trending templates updated constantly. AI Clipper turns long-form content into shorts automatically. Auto-captioning with animated text styles. You can go from raw footage to published in 10-15 minutes. Descript takes 30-40 minutes for the same output.

**AI features:** Different strengths. Descript's AI is about audio: Studio Sound cleans up rough recordings, filler word removal strips the "ums" and "uhs," voice cloning handles overdubs. CapCut's AI is about visuals: generative video, auto-reframe, AI masking for background removal.

**Mobile:** CapCut has an excellent mobile app. Shoot and edit in the same workflow. Descript is desktop and web only. If mobile editing matters to you, this is a dealbreaker.

## FAQ

**Is CapCut really free?** Yes, and it's very generous. Full editor, 1080p, no watermark. You only hit limits when you want 4K exports or AI-generative features.

**Can CapCut handle podcasts?** It can edit audio, but without transcript-based editing, you're scrubbing a timeline for 60 minutes. Descript is significantly more efficient for this.

**Does Descript work for social media?** You can use it, but it lacks the template library, trending effects, and mobile editing that make CapCut fast for short-form content.
