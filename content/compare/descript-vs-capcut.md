## What Is Descript?

Descript is an AI-powered audio and video editor built around one breakthrough idea: editing media by editing text. When you import a video or audio file, Descript automatically transcribes it. From there, you edit the transcript -- delete a sentence, and the corresponding audio and video disappear. Rearrange paragraphs, and the timeline follows. For anyone who works with spoken-word content, this feels almost magical the first time you use it.

The company has steadily expanded beyond its transcription roots. **Studio Sound** cleans up rough audio recordings to sound like they were captured in a professional booth. **Eye Contact** uses AI to make speakers appear to look directly at the camera, even when they were reading notes off-screen. **Filler word removal** automatically strips out "ums," "uhs," and "you knows" with a single click -- a task that would take hours of manual editing.

Descript offers five tiers in 2026. The **Free** plan gives you 1 hour of transcription and 5 minutes of AI speech with 720p watermarked exports. **Hobbyist** ($16/month billed annually) bumps that to 10 hours of transcription, 30 minutes of AI speech, and 1080p exports. **Creator** ($24/month) is the sweet spot for most users: 30 hours of transcription, 2 hours of AI speech, 4K exports, and full access to advanced AI features. **Business** ($50/month) adds team collaboration, brand-wide voice cloning, multi-language translation, and priority support. Enterprise pricing is custom.

## What Is CapCut?

CapCut is ByteDance's video editing platform -- the same company behind TikTok -- and that lineage shows in everything about the product. It was designed from the ground up for the social media era: fast edits, trending templates, vertical video, and one-tap effects that make content look polished in minutes rather than hours.

What makes CapCut remarkable is how much you get for free. The free plan includes the full editing suite, 1080p watermark-free exports, an extensive library of effects, transitions, music, and auto-captioning. There's no other video editor at any price point that offers this much at zero cost. The catch is subtle -- CapCut is designed to keep you inside the TikTok ecosystem, and some premium templates and AI features are gated behind the Pro plan.

**CapCut Pro** costs $9.99/month (or $79.99/year on some platforms, though pricing varies by region). Pro unlocks 4K/60fps/HDR exports, 100GB cloud storage, generative AI video from text prompts, the AI Clipper for automatically turning long-form content into shorts, advanced AI masking for background removal, and Studio Audio for pro-grade noise reduction and vocal isolation. A **Team** plan at roughly $24.99/month adds collaborative features. CapCut works everywhere -- desktop app, web browser, and mobile -- with projects syncing across devices.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### Pricing

CapCut wins on price at every tier. The free plan alone is more capable than Descript's paid Hobbyist plan in many respects (1080p exports without watermarks versus 720p with watermarks on Descript Free). For paid plans, CapCut Pro at $9.99/month undercuts Descript Creator at $24/month by more than half.

However, pricing only tells part of the story. Descript's value comes from features CapCut simply doesn't offer -- transcript-based editing, AI filler word removal, and Studio Sound audio enhancement. If those features save you hours per week, $24/month is a bargain.

### Features

**Descript wins on:** transcript-based editing (no competitor matches this), automatic filler word removal, Studio Sound audio cleanup, Eye Contact correction, AI voice cloning for overdubs, multi-language transcription in 25+ languages, and collaborative editing with comments tied to specific moments in the transcript.

**CapCut wins on:** template library (thousands of trending templates updated constantly), mobile editing (Descript has no mobile app), speed of social media workflows (10-15 minutes from raw footage to published versus 30-40 minutes in Descript for the same content), auto-captioning with animated text styles, AI Clipper for automatic short-form repurposing, generative AI b-roll creation, and the sheer breadth of visual effects, filters, and transitions.

### Ease of Use

CapCut is easier to pick up, especially for anyone who has used a mobile video editor before. The interface follows familiar patterns -- timeline at the bottom, preview on top, effects panel on the side. Templates let you create polished content even on your first day. The mobile app is excellent, letting you shoot and edit in the same workflow.

Descript has a steeper learning curve because the transcript-based paradigm is unfamiliar. Most people need 30-60 minutes to understand that you edit the text, not the timeline. Once that clicks, the workflow becomes incredibly efficient for spoken-word content. But if your content is primarily visual rather than verbal, Descript's core advantage doesn't apply.

## Who Should Use What?

**Pick Descript if:** you produce podcasts, interviews, educational videos, or any content where spoken words are the primary element. You want to edit by reading and deleting text rather than scrubbing through a timeline. You need professional audio cleanup without expensive gear. You work in a team that needs to review and comment on content collaboratively.

**Pick CapCut if:** you create content for TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, or other social platforms. Speed matters more than precision -- you need to turn around content in minutes, not hours. You want access to trending templates and effects. You edit on mobile as often as desktop. Budget is a concern and you want maximum capability for minimum cost (or free).

## FAQ

### Can I use Descript for social media content?

You can, but it's not optimized for it. Descript lacks the template library, trending effects, and mobile editing that make CapCut so fast for short-form social content. Where Descript shines for social is repurposing long-form content -- edit a full podcast episode, then export key clips.

### Is CapCut really free?

Yes, and it's very generous. The free plan includes the full editing suite with 1080p watermark-free exports. You only hit limits when you want 4K exports, AI-generative features, or cloud storage. Most casual creators never need to upgrade.

### Can CapCut handle podcasts?

CapCut can edit audio, but it lacks Descript's killer feature: transcript-based editing. Editing a 60-minute podcast by scrubbing a timeline is painful compared to reading a transcript and deleting paragraphs. For podcast production, Descript is significantly more efficient.

### Does Descript have a mobile app?

No. Descript is desktop and web only. This is a meaningful limitation if mobile editing is part of your workflow.

### Which has better AI features?

Different strengths. Descript's AI is focused on audio quality (Studio Sound, filler word removal, voice cloning). CapCut's AI is focused on visual content (generative video, auto-reframe, AI masking). Neither is objectively better -- it depends on what type of content you create.
