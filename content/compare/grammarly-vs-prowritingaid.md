## The details

**Pricing:** Both cost $30/mo on monthly billing. Annual plans bring it to $12/mo (Grammarly) and $10/mo (ProWritingAid). ProWritingAid's lifetime deal at $399 pays for itself in about 3.3 years vs the annual plan. Grammarly has no lifetime option.

**Grammar and speed:** Both catch errors well. Grammarly is faster at real-time corrections and works in more places out of the box (Slack, Discord, Notion). ProWritingAid sometimes catches style issues Grammarly misses, especially around sentence structure.

**Writing reports:** This is where ProWritingAid dominates. 25+ reports covering readability, sentence length variation, overused words, passive voice, dialogue analysis, and pacing. Grammarly gives you a performance score and tone detection. Nothing close to the same depth.

**AI features:** Grammarly's AI generates text, rewrites sentences, and adjusts tone. ProWritingAid offers "AI Sparks" with shorter suggestions, 3-5 per day. Grammarly's AI is more capable for generation. ProWritingAid's analysis reports are more useful for improvement.

**Integrations:** Grammarly integrates with more platforms (Slack, Discord, Notion, plus browser and desktop). ProWritingAid covers the essentials (browser, Word, Google Docs) and uniquely supports Scrivener, which matters for fiction writers.

## FAQ

**Is Grammarly more accurate than ProWritingAid?** For basic grammar, both are very accurate. Grammarly catches errors faster in real time. ProWritingAid surfaces deeper style issues around sentence structure and readability.

**Is the ProWritingAid lifetime deal worth it?** At $399, it pays for itself in 3.3 years vs the $120/yr annual plan. If you plan to write seriously for more than three years, yes.

**Can I use both?** Some writers do. Grammarly's browser extension for daily writing, ProWritingAid's reports for longer pieces. Paying for both gets expensive unless you use ProWritingAid's lifetime deal.
