Quick Verdict#

Choosing between Beehiiv and Substack? Here’s the short version: Substack is the simpler choice – start writing immediately, tap into a built-in audience network, and pay nothing until you monetize (then 10% of revenue). Beehiiv is the growth-focused choice – more monetization options, advanced analytics, and full control over your brand, with flat monthly pricing that doesn’t scale with your revenue. The right pick depends on whether you prioritize simplicity or control.

What Is Beehiiv?#

Beehiiv launched in 2022, founded by early Morning Brew employees who understood exactly what growth-focused newsletter operators need. The platform was built from the ground up for creators who treat their newsletter as a business, not just a writing hobby.

The standout feature is monetization flexibility. Unlike Substack’s single revenue model (paid subscriptions), Beehiiv offers multiple paths to income. The native Ad Network connects you with brand sponsors – you can monetize your free newsletter without ever charging readers. You can also sell digital products (PDFs, templates, courses) directly through the platform with zero commission from Beehiiv. And yes, you can run paid subscriptions too, keeping 100% of the revenue (minus payment processing fees).

Growth tools are where Beehiiv really separates itself. Boosts is a paid recommendation marketplace where you can acquire subscribers by paying other newsletter creators to recommend you (or earn money by recommending others). The referral program lets readers unlock rewards for sharing your newsletter. Add in advanced segmentation, A/B testing, automation workflows, and detailed analytics with UTM tracking, and you have a platform that’s built for scale. The Scale plan starts at $49/month for up to 1,000 subscribers (pricing increases with subscriber count), while the Max plan at $109/month adds white-labeling, priority support, and advanced AI tools.

What Is Substack?#

Substack is the platform that kickstarted the modern newsletter renaissance. Since 2017, it has attracted some of the biggest names in journalism, politics, and culture by offering the simplest possible deal: write, publish, and we’ll handle everything else.

The zero-friction model is Substack’s greatest strength. There are no plans to choose, no features to configure, no upfront costs. You sign up, start writing, and publish. If you want to charge readers for subscriptions, Substack takes a 10% cut plus Stripe processing fees (roughly 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction). If you never charge, you never pay anything.

Substack’s network effects are genuinely powerful. The platform has evolved beyond email into a social reading experience with Notes (a Twitter-like feed for short posts), Chat (community discussions), and Recommendations (cross-promotion between writers). When established Substack writers recommend your newsletter, you can gain hundreds of subscribers overnight. The platform also hosts podcasts and video, making it a multi-format publishing tool. Substack’s reader app has millions of active users browsing and discovering new writers – a built-in audience that no other newsletter platform can match.

Head-to-Head Comparison#

Pricing#

This is where the comparison gets nuanced. Substack appears free, but the 10% revenue share adds up fast.

Example: A newsletter with 5,000 paid subscribers at $10/month generates $50,000/month. Substack takes $5,000/month ($60,000/year). On Beehiiv, you’d pay a flat monthly fee based on subscriber count – roughly $99-$159/month for that tier, keeping over $58,000 more per year.

At lower volumes, Substack wins. If you have 100 paid subscribers at $5/month, Substack takes $50/month. Beehiiv’s Scale plan costs $49/month regardless. The crossover point is roughly when your paid subscription revenue exceeds $500-600/month.

Features#

Beehiiv wins on: analytics (detailed UTM tracking, subscriber scoring, engagement funnels), monetization options (ads, digital products, Boosts), design customization, automation workflows, and A/B testing.

Substack wins on: simplicity, built-in audience network (Notes, reader app), community features (Chat, discussion threads), multi-format publishing (podcasts + video), and discoverability through the Substack network.

Ease of Use#

Substack wins decisively here. The editor is distraction-free, there are no settings to configure, and publishing takes seconds. It feels like writing in a journal.

Beehiiv is more capable but requires more setup. You’ll spend time configuring segments, designing templates, setting up automations, and learning the analytics dashboard. It’s not difficult, but there’s a real onboarding period before you’re using the platform to its potential.

Who Should Use What?#

Pick Beehiiv if: you’re building a newsletter business and want maximum control over monetization, branding, and growth. You plan to monetize through ads and sponsorships (not just paid subscriptions). Your revenue will exceed $500/month from subscriptions, making Substack’s 10% cut more expensive than Beehiiv’s flat fee.

Pick Substack if: you’re a writer first and a business operator second. You value simplicity and want to focus on writing, not platform configuration. You want access to Substack’s built-in audience network for discoverability. You’re just starting out and don’t want to pay anything until you have paying readers.

FAQ#

Does Substack really take 10% forever?#

Yes. Unlike a SaaS fee that stays flat, Substack’s 10% scales with your revenue indefinitely. At $1,000/month in subscription revenue, you pay $100. At $10,000/month, you pay $1,000. There’s no cap, no volume discount, and no way to negotiate it down.

Can I switch from Substack to Beehiiv?#

Yes, and Beehiiv makes this easy with a direct Substack import tool. You can migrate your subscriber list, archive posts, and even preserve your custom domain. Many high-profile creators have made this switch as their revenue grew.

Which is better for free newsletters?#

Both work well. Substack’s network effects give free newsletters better organic discovery. Beehiiv’s Ad Network lets free newsletters monetize through sponsorships without ever charging readers – a revenue path Substack doesn’t offer.

Can I use Beehiiv’s ad network from day one?#

You need to be on the Scale plan ($49/month) or higher and meet minimum subscriber thresholds to access the ad network. Most new creators start with the free Launch plan and upgrade once they’ve built an initial audience.

Our Recommendation#

If you’re just starting a newsletter and want zero risk, Substack is the obvious choice. You pay nothing, the writing experience is beautiful, and the network can accelerate early growth.

Once your newsletter becomes a business – especially if subscription revenue passes $500/month or you want to monetize through ads – Beehiiv becomes the smarter financial choice. The flat pricing model, multiple revenue streams, and advanced growth tools are built for creators who think of their newsletter as a company.

Many successful creators start on Substack for the simplicity and network, then migrate to Beehiiv when they outgrow it. That’s a perfectly valid strategy.
