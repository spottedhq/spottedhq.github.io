Quick Verdict#

Choosing between Semrush and Ahrefs? Here’s the short version: Semrush is the broader marketing platform — SEO, PPC, social media, and content marketing in one dashboard. Ahrefs is the pure SEO powerhouse — the best backlink index in the industry, cleaner interface, and recently more accessible with its new $29/month Starter plan. Pick Semrush for all-in-one marketing. Pick Ahrefs for focused SEO research.

What Is Semrush?#

Semrush started as a keyword research tool in 2008 and has evolved into the most comprehensive digital marketing platform available. It covers SEO, PPC advertising, social media management, content marketing, and competitive analysis — all from a single subscription.

The platform’s strength is breadth. The Pro plan alone includes keyword research, site auditing, rank tracking (500 keywords), backlink analysis, on-page SEO recommendations, and competitor analysis. Move up to Guru ($249.95/month) and you unlock content marketing tools — a topic research engine, SEO writing assistant, and content audit functionality. The Business plan ($499.95/month) adds API access, share of voice metrics, and white-label reports.

In late 2025, Semrush launched Semrush One, bundling the core SEO toolkit with a new AI Visibility Toolkit designed to track brand mentions and visibility across AI-generated search results. As Google and other search engines increasingly surface AI answers, this forward-looking feature gives Semrush a unique angle that Ahrefs has not yet matched.

What Is Ahrefs?#

Ahrefs is a Singapore-based SEO toolset known for having the largest backlink index on the web. It crawls over 8 billion pages daily, and its link data is widely considered the most comprehensive and freshest available — making it the go-to tool for link building and backlink analysis.

Beyond backlinks, Ahrefs offers keyword research (Keywords Explorer), rank tracking, site auditing, and content gap analysis. The interface is cleaner and more focused than Semrush’s — it does fewer things, but does them with exceptional depth.

A major shift happened in January 2026 when Ahrefs introduced the Starter plan at $29/month, slashing the entry price by 70%. Previously, the cheapest Ahrefs plan was $99/month (now $129/month as Lite), which priced out freelancers and small site owners. The Starter plan includes 1 project, 100 tracked keywords, and 5K crawl credits per month — enough for a single site or niche blog.

Ahrefs’ new Starter plan has dramatically changed the comparison. At $29/month versus Semrush’s $139.95/month entry point, Ahrefs is now far more accessible for individual site owners. However, the Starter plan is intentionally limited — one project, basic crawl credits, no historical data.

At comparable feature levels, Semrush Pro ($139.95) versus Ahrefs Lite ($129) are close in price, but Semrush includes PPC and content tools that Ahrefs does not offer at any price.

Hidden costs to watch: Ahrefs charges $40-80/month for additional users depending on your plan, and daily rank tracking (vs. weekly) costs an extra $50/month. Semrush includes more user seats in its base plans.

Backlink Analysis#

Ahrefs wins. Its backlink index is the largest and most frequently updated. The referring domains data, anchor text analysis, and link intersect tool are all best-in-class. If backlink research is your primary use case, there is no better tool.

Semrush has a solid backlink database that has improved significantly in recent years, but it still trails Ahrefs in raw coverage and freshness. For most SEO work, Semrush’s backlink data is sufficient, but link building specialists will notice the difference.

Keyword Research#

Both tools are excellent here, with slightly different strengths.

Semrush provides keyword difficulty scores, search volume, CPC data, SERP features, and competitive density. Its Keyword Magic Tool is particularly good at expanding seed keywords into large, organized lists. It also shows PPC competition data, which Ahrefs does not.

Ahrefs Keywords Explorer offers similar metrics with the addition of click data — showing not just search volume but estimated clicks, which accounts for zero-click searches. This gives a more realistic picture of traffic potential.

Content and Marketing Tools#

Semrush wins by a wide margin. The Guru plan includes a full content marketing toolkit: topic research, SEO content templates, a writing assistant that scores content in real time, post tracking, and content auditing. It also offers social media scheduling, PPC research, and advertising analysis.

Ahrefs is laser-focused on SEO. It does not offer PPC tools, social media management, or a built-in content writing assistant. If you need those capabilities, you will need separate tools alongside Ahrefs.

Ease of Use#

Ahrefs has a cleaner, more intuitive interface. The navigation is straightforward, reports load quickly, and the data presentation is less cluttered. New users typically find Ahrefs easier to learn.

Semrush packs more features into its dashboard, which means more complexity. The learning curve is steeper, and it can feel overwhelming when you first log in. However, once you know where things are, the centralized access to SEO, PPC, and content tools saves time versus switching between platforms.

Who Should Use What?#

Pick Semrush if: you are a marketing team or agency that needs SEO, PPC research, content marketing, and social media in one platform. The all-in-one approach eliminates the cost and friction of juggling multiple tools. The Pro plan at $139.95/month is expensive but replaces several subscriptions. The new AI Visibility Toolkit also makes it forward-looking for brands concerned about visibility in AI-generated search results.

Pick Ahrefs if: you are an SEO specialist, link builder, or site owner who wants the best backlink data and keyword research without the extra marketing features. The new Starter plan at $29/month makes Ahrefs accessible to freelancers and small site owners for the first time. For pure SEO work, Ahrefs delivers more depth per dollar.

FAQ#

Is Semrush worth the price?#

If you use its full feature set — SEO, PPC, content, social — then yes. Semrush replaces multiple tools, which can offset the higher price. If you only need SEO, Ahrefs offers comparable (often better) SEO data at a lower price point.

Can I try Ahrefs for free?#

Ahrefs Webmaster Tools is free but limited to data about your own verified site. There is no free trial of paid plans. The $29/month Starter plan is the lowest-cost way to access Ahrefs’ full dataset.

Which has better keyword data?#

Both are excellent. Semrush has a slightly larger keyword database and includes PPC metrics. Ahrefs offers click data that gives more realistic traffic estimates. For most SEO work, either tool provides sufficient keyword intelligence.

Can I switch from one to the other?#

Both export data to CSV, so you can move keyword lists, backlink reports, and rank tracking data between platforms. However, historical data and custom reports do not transfer — you would be starting fresh with the new tool’s data.

Our Recommendation#

For solo site owners and freelancers, start with Ahrefs Starter at $29/month. It gives you access to the best backlink index and solid keyword research at a price that was not available a year ago. Upgrade to Lite or Standard as your needs grow.

For marketing teams and agencies, Semrush Pro at $139.95/month is the better investment. The all-in-one platform eliminates tool sprawl, and the content marketing and PPC features justify the premium over Ahrefs. Consider Guru if content optimization is a core workflow.
